\begin{abstract}
  Cyclic graph representations of $\lambda$-terms have traditionally relied on extensions to the pure calculus. We give a least-fixpoint semantics for the $\lambda$-calculus by defining a monotone approximation order on $\lambda$-terms. The semantics is parameterized by a normalization $f$, whose coinductive reading is the standard, possibly infinite tree; we take instead the least fixpoint $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ of the one-step observation $\out$ it induces. Weak head and head normalization are the two instances. When evaluated by tabling, $\Omega$ terminates as $\bot$, and pure $\lambda$-terms can build and transform potentially infinite cyclic graphs.

\end{abstract}

\keywords{least fixpoint, head reduction, B\"ohm trees, rational trees, cyclic data,
  \texorpdfstring{$\lambda$-calculus}{lambda-calculus}}

\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
The tree of a $\lambda$-term, its B\"ohm tree or L\'evy--Longo tree, is obtained by iterating head
reduction at every exposed position \cite{barendregt, levy-longo}. Head reduction is the shared
engine, and it only ever \emph{unfolds}, so on a cyclic term it runs forever. Three pure terms expose
two obstacles to recovering a finite answer,
\[
  \Omega, \qquad r = Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\,0), \qquad \mathtt{map}\;\mathtt{succ}\;r,
\]
the diverging combination, the cyclic stream of zeros, and an ordinary $\mathtt{map}$ that increments
it. The $\mathtt{map}$ is the textbook one, with nothing cycle-aware in it,
\[
  \mathtt{map}\,f = Y\,\big(\lambda \mathit{self}.\,\lambda \mathit{xs}.\,
       \mathit{xs}\,(\lambda h.\,\lambda t.\, \mathtt{cons}\,(f\,h)\,(\mathit{self}\,t))\,\mathtt{nil}\big);
\]
see Appendix~\ref{app:examples} for $Y$, $\mathtt{cons}$, $\mathtt{succ}$, and the complete
definitions.

The first obstacle is identity. $\Omega = (\lambda x.\,x\,x)(\lambda x.\,x\,x)$ head reduces to
itself, so its tree should be the meaningless $\bot$; but recognising that head reduction has returned
to its starting term means deciding identity on values, and on a function space the only identity is
$\beta$-equality, which is undecidable. The second is representation. The stream $r$ is rational, one
repeating cell, and should have a finite cyclic graph, yet head reduction enumerates it forever, and a
pure calculus cannot even build the cycle: a circular structure normally needs an explicit recursion
binder ($\mathtt{letrec}$), mutable references, or an observable-sharing primitive
\cite{ariola-klop, gill}. The two obstacles meet in $\mathtt{map}\,\mathtt{succ}\,r$: head reduction
diverges, yet the result is again a finite cyclic list and should terminate, and the existing
mechanisms can neither detect the repeated recursive call nor fold the result.

We resolve both by reading the tree not as the unfolding of head reduction but as its \emph{least
fixpoint}. We give the partial trees a monotone approximation order and identify positions up to a
decidable congruence; over this order the construction is parametric in a \emph{normalization} $f$,
with weak head normalization yielding the L\'evy--Longo tree and head normalization the B\"ohm tree.
The least fixpoint $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ of the one-step observation $\out$ that $f$ induces is
the same tree that head reduction enumerates, but it folds a guarded cycle into a finite rational
graph and settles an unproductive cycle at $\bot$:
$\Omega$ is $\bot$ in finite time, $r$ folds to a node pointing to itself, and
$\mathtt{map}\,\mathtt{succ}\,r$ folds to a finite circular list of ones, all where head reduction
diverges, with nothing added to the calculus. We have implemented an interpreter that validates these
behaviours\anon[.]{ \cite{firstorderlambda}.}

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The tree of an admissible normalization $f$ as the least fixpoint $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ of a
        monotone operator over the approximation order, parametric in $f$, with weak head and head
        normalization the two standard instances (Section~\ref{sec:lfp}).
  \item Folding: taken up to a sound position congruence, $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ is a finite rational
        graph wherever the term has finitely many position classes, and decides an unproductive cycle
        such as $\Omega$ as $\bot$ (Section~\ref{sec:lfp}).
  \item As an application, structural computation over cyclic data with nothing added to the calculus
        (Section~\ref{sec:application}): $Y$ builds the cycle, the congruence folds it, and an ordinary
        recursive function over it terminates where head reduction diverges.
\end{itemize}

% =====================================================================
\section{The Least Fixpoint}\label{sec:lfp}
% =====================================================================
All three defects come from one choice: the tree is read as the \emph{unfolding} of $f$, a
greatest-fixpoint (coinductive) object, so a cycle unfolds without end. We change only this. We give
the trees a monotone order and read the tree of a term as the \emph{least} fixpoint of $f$. This
section fixes the order, says which normalizations $f$ admit such a fixpoint, and proves that the two
standard ones do.

\begin{definition}[Partial trees and the approximation order]\label{def:order}
Let $\Sigma$ be the signature with nullary symbols $\bot$ and $\mathsf{Var}_i$ ($i\in\mathbb{N}$), a
unary $\mathsf{Lam}$, and a binary $\mathsf{App}$. A \emph{partial tree} is an arity-respecting map
from a nonempty prefix-closed set of paths to $\Sigma$. The \emph{approximation order} on the set
$D$ of partial trees is $t\sqsubseteq u$ iff $\operatorname{dom}(t)\subseteq\operatorname{dom}(u)$
and, at every $p\in\operatorname{dom}(t)$, either $t(p)=u(p)$ or $t(p)=\bot$. Then $(D,\sqsubseteq)$
is a pointed $\omega$-complete partial order: its least element is the one-node tree $\bot$, and the
least upper bound of a $\sqsubseteq$-chain is taken pathwise. Thus $\bot$ marks a subtree not yet
produced, and refinement fills the hole.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Normalization, its observation, and the operator]\label{def:operator}
A \emph{normalization} $f$ drives a term toward a normal form of a chosen kind by contracting one
head redex at a time: a deterministic partial function on $\Lambda$, the contraction of the head
redex, whose domain, the non-normal terms, is decidable; the terms outside the domain are the
\emph{$f$-normal} forms (the weak head and the head normal forms,
Propositions~\ref{prop:llt}--\ref{prop:bt}). It is \emph{admissible} when, moreover, the outermost
constructor of an $f$-normal term, one of $\mathsf{Var}_i$, $\mathsf{Lam}(N)$, or $\mathsf{App}(P,Q)$,
is a function of its syntax, never the detection that a subterm diverges.

An admissible $f$ makes $\Lambda$ a coalgebra: it gives each term one \emph{observation} $\out(M)$,
the successor state $f(M)$ when $M$ is not $f$-normal, and otherwise the outermost constructor with
its immediate sub-positions. Read coinductively, $\out$ unfolds the standard, possibly infinite tree,
the B\"ohm or L\'evy--Longo tree, which never finishes on a cyclic term. We read it inductively
instead. The observation induces the operator $T_f$ on $[\Lambda\to D]$, ordered pointwise by
Definition~\ref{def:order},
\[
  T_f(g)(M) =
  \begin{cases}
    g\big(f(M)\big) & M \text{ is not } f\text{-normal},\\
    \mathsf{Var}_i & M \text{ is } f\text{-normal with head } \mathsf{Var}_i,\\
    \mathsf{Lam}\big(g\,N\big) & M \text{ is } f\text{-normal of the form } \mathsf{Lam}(N),\\
    \mathsf{App}\big(g\,P,\, g\,Q\big) & M \text{ is } f\text{-normal of the form } \mathsf{App}(P,Q),
  \end{cases}
\]
whose greatest fixpoint is that coinductive unfolding and whose \emph{least} fixpoint, written
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$, is our semantics.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Soundness]\label{prop:sound}
For an admissible normalization $f$, the operator $T_f$ is monotone and $\omega$-continuous, so by
the Kleene fixed-point theorem it has a least fixpoint
\[
  \operatorname{lfp}(\out) \;:=\; \operatorname{lfp}(T_f) \;=\; \textstyle\bigsqcup_{k\in\mathbb{N}} T_f^{\,k}(\bot),
\]
the tree of $M$, and $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M)=\bot$ exactly when $f$ does not terminate at $M$.
The greatest fixpoint, the coinductive unfolding, instead loops at such a term; taking the least
fixpoint is what settles an unproductive cycle at $\bot$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Whether $M$ is $f$-normal is decidable by hypothesis, so the clause of $T_f$ at $M$ is selected
independently of $g$; within each clause $g$ occurs only positively and at finitely many arguments.
Hence $g\sqsubseteq g'$ gives $T_f(g)\sqsubseteq T_f(g')$, so $T_f$ is monotone, and $T_f$ preserves
least upper bounds of $\sqsubseteq$-chains, so it is $\omega$-continuous; the Kleene theorem then
gives the displayed least fixpoint. By induction on $k$, $T_f^{\,k}(\bot)(M)\neq\bot$ iff some head
reduction of fewer than $k$ steps takes $M$ to an $f$-normal term; hence
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M)=\bot$ iff no such reduction exists, that is, iff $f$ diverges at $M$.
\end{proof}

So far the tree is read one position at a time. Folding is a second, independent ingredient: a
congruence under which a re-entry is recognised.

\begin{definition}[Sound congruence]\label{def:cong}
A decidable equivalence $\approx$ on $\Lambda$ is a \emph{sound congruence} when it respects the
descent: if $M\approx M'$ then $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M)$ and $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M')$ carry the
same outermost label, and the subpositions the readout descends into, the contractum $f(M)$ when $M$
is not $f$-normal and the immediate subterms of the exposed constructor when it is, are pairwise
$\approx$-related to those of $M'$. Syntactic, de Bruijn, identity is the finest such congruence.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Folding]\label{prop:fold}
A sound congruence is contained in tree equality: $M\approx M'$ implies
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M)=\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M')$, so $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ factors through the
quotient $\Lambda/{\approx}$ and $T_f$ descends to $[\Lambda/{\approx}\to D]$ with the same least
fixpoint. If the positions reachable from $M$ meet finitely many $\approx$-classes, then
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)(M)$ solves finitely many equations, one per class, and is a finite rational
graph; a \emph{guarded} cycle, a class recurring strictly below itself, folds to a back-reference,
where the unfolding of $f$ would descend forever.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Tree equality is the greatest fixpoint of the behavioural operator that relates two trees sharing an
outermost label and having pairwise-related children. The descent condition makes $\approx$ a
post-fixpoint of this operator, so $\approx$ is contained in tree equality by coinduction; this gives
the equality and the factoring, and $T_f$ descends to classes because $f$ and the exposure respect
$\approx$. When $M$ reaches finitely many classes, the fixpoint is determined by one equation per
class, whose solution is the finite rational graph; a class occurring below itself is a guarded
cycle, folded to its back-reference.
\end{proof}

The two standard tree semantics are the two admissible normalizations.

\begin{proposition}[Weak head normalization gives the L\'evy--Longo tree]\label{prop:llt}
Let $f_{\mathrm w}$ contract the head redex but not under a leading $\lambda$, with the weak head
normal forms, $\mathsf{Lam}(N)$ and $\mathsf{Var}_i\,M_1\cdots M_k$, as its normal terms. Then
$f_{\mathrm w}$ is admissible, so its observation $\out_{\mathrm w}$ has a least fixpoint
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm w})$ by Proposition~\ref{prop:sound}; it is the L\'evy--Longo tree,
and it is $\bot$ at $M$ iff $M$ has no weak head normal form.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
A weak head normal form is recognised by inspecting the head of the spine, an outermost $\lambda$ or
a variable head, so the predicate is decidable; the head contraction is a deterministic syntactic
partial function; and the outermost constructor of a normal form is read off its syntax, a positive
test. Thus $f_{\mathrm w}$ is admissible. Its readout labels each position by the weak head normal
form reached there, which is the L\'evy--Longo tree by definition, and its meaningless terms, those
without a weak head normal form, are where $\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm w})=\bot$ by
Proposition~\ref{prop:sound}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Head normalization gives the B\"ohm tree]\label{prop:bt}
Let $f_{\mathrm h}$ contract the head redex, now also under leading $\lambda$s, with the head normal
forms $\lambda x_1\cdots\lambda x_n.\,y\,M_1\cdots M_k$ as its normal terms. Then $f_{\mathrm h}$ is
admissible, so its observation $\out_{\mathrm h}$ has a least fixpoint
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$; it is the B\"ohm tree, and it is $\bot$ at $M$ iff $M$ has no
head normal form.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
A head normal form is recognised by peeling the leading $\lambda$s and testing that the head of the
remaining spine is a variable, again decidable; the head contraction under leading $\lambda$s is
deterministic and syntactic; and the exposure is the same positive read of the outermost
constructor. Thus $f_{\mathrm h}$ is admissible, Proposition~\ref{prop:sound} gives the fixpoint, it
is the B\"ohm tree, and $\bot$ marks the terms with no head normal form.
\end{proof}

Admissibility is exactly what the Berarducci tree lacks: it retains a $\bot$-headed application
instead of collapsing it, which requires testing that the head has no normal form, a negative
condition, so its operator is not monotone and has no least fixpoint.

The three behaviours of the introduction are now the three cases above: $\Omega$ is the divergent
normalization of Proposition~\ref{prop:sound}, reading out as $\bot$; a productive term yields its
tree; and a guarded cycle folds by Proposition~\ref{prop:fold}, where the unfolding diverges.
Section~\ref{sec:implementation} computes $\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ by tabling and validates the
examples of Appendix~\ref{app:examples}\anon[.]{ \cite{firstorderlambda}.}

\section{Structural Computation over Cyclic Data}\label{sec:application}
% =====================================================================
The least fixpoint turns the pure $\lambda$-calculus into a setting for structural computation over
cyclic data, with nothing added to the calculus.

Building the datum is already the new step. The cyclic stream of zeros, $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$,
is written purely as $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$. Under the unfolding it has no finite form; under
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ it has finitely many positions up to the congruence, so it folds to a finite
circular list, a single cell whose tail points back to itself. A circular linked list from a pure
term is exactly what the standard reading cannot build: here $Y$ produces the repetition and the
congruence folds it.

A recursive query over the datum is then an ordinary $\lambda$-function, and it terminates for the
same reason the tree does. A naive walk looking for $\mathtt{nil}$ recurses down the stream; its
recursive calls fall into finitely many position classes, so the least fixpoint folds them and
returns $\bot$ (no $\mathtt{nil}$ is ever reached, an unproductive cycle) in finite time, where the
reduction would build a fresh call forever. The textbook $\mathtt{map}$, whose recursive call sits
under a $\mathtt{cons}$ and is therefore guarded, folds the cyclic input into a finite circular list
with every element transformed, where the reduction would map the infinite stream forever. On a
finite list the same $\mathtt{map}$ is the ordinary one. The implementation reproduces all of this,
including $\Omega$ and $Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$ reading out as $\bot$\anon[.]{~\cite{firstorderlambda}.}

% =====================================================================
\section{Implementation}\label{sec:implementation}
% =====================================================================
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ is computed by \emph{tabling}, the standard least-fixpoint evaluation that
completes the cyclic and left-recursive queries on which plain resolution diverges
\cite{vanemden-kowalski, tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}. Each formal notion has a direct counterpart, and
this correspondence is the plan the accompanying artifact follows\anon[.]{~\cite{firstorderlambda}.}

\paragraph{A position is a tabled call; the congruence is the tabling key.}
Computing the tree at a position is a call, and its answer is memoised in a table. Two positions are
the \emph{same} call exactly when they are $\approx$-equal, so the congruence
(Definition~\ref{def:cong}) is the tabling key. The finest key, syntactic de Bruijn identity, is
realised by hash-consing (interning), an $O(1)$ pointer test; a coarser sound $\approx$ is a coarser
call equivalence that tables more positions together and so folds more.

\paragraph{$\operatorname{lfp}(\out)$ is the table's least fixpoint; a guarded cycle folds on re-entry.}
Each answer is built from $\bot$ upward. A position re-entered while its call is still in progress
does not unfold; it consumes the call's current answer, a back-reference to the node being built. The
table holds one entry per $\approx$-class, so when the reachable classes are finite it saturates into
a finite rational graph, the guarded cycle folded to that back-reference and printed explicitly. This
is ``the least fixpoint over a finite quotient'' made operational.

\paragraph{An unproductive cycle is a call that never produces.}
A position whose head evaluation re-enters its own call before exposing a constructor, such as
$\Omega$, has a tabled answer that never improves past $\bot$, so it is decided as $\bot$ in finite
time, where untabled head reduction loops. Guarded and unproductive cycles are then one mechanism:
re-entry consults the table, which holds a constructor in the first case and $\bot$ in the second.

\paragraph{Two readings, one engine.}
The two readings are a policy flag on the same tabling: the least fixpoint, which folds a guarded
re-entry, and a finite-budget first iterate, which cuts it to a distinct leaf kept apart from the
meaningless $\bot$.

\paragraph{The congruence is pluggable.}
The tabling key ranges over the spectrum of Definition~\ref{def:cong}: interning for syntactic
identity (the finest); a union-find with congruence closure, an e-graph, for the inductive end,
folding whatever finitely many asserted tree-equal pairs generate; a bisimulation on the demanded
descent for the coinductive end, folding exactly the rational fragment; and a dead-subterm
canonicalisation that tables together positions differing only in a dead argument, which no
congruence closure reaches.

\paragraph{Normalization flag and termination guard.}
Which normalization is a flag, whether to reduce under a leading $\lambda$: weak head normalization
(the L\'evy--Longo tree) and head normalization (the B\"ohm tree). A non-rational term raises
infinitely many calls and the table never saturates; a reduction budget bounds head reduction so such
a term surfaces as an error rather than hanging.

\paragraph{Realisation and the examples.}
The present artifact realises the table by knot-tying, the special case in which the call's
in-progress node is itself the table entry and the key is interning; hosting the same table in a
reusable least-fixpoint memoised property would decouple it from the readout and admit a coarser key.
The examples are tests: $\Omega$ and $Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$ read out as $\bot$, $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\,0)$
folds to a finite cyclic cell, $\mathtt{map}\,\mathtt{succ}$ over it folds to the cyclic stream of
ones, and the naive walk terminates as $\bot$ where head reduction loops
(Appendix~\ref{app:examples}).

\appendix
\section{The Example Terms}\label{app:examples}
% =====================================================================
Every example is a closed pure $\lambda$-term, built from Scott-encoded lists, the Church numeral
$0$, and the fixpoint combinator $Y$, with no recursion binder added to the calculus. These are the
terms the implementation runs\anon[.]{~\cite{firstorderlambda}.} For each we recall its B\"ohm tree,
the original semantics, and contrast how it is computed: head normalization $f_{\mathrm h}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:bt}) enumerates the tree by unfolding and diverges on a cyclic term, whereas
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$ yields the same tree, folded.

The combinators are the identity, self-application, and the fixpoint combinator:
\[
  I = \lambda x.\, x, \qquad
  \omega = \lambda x.\, x\,x, \qquad
  Y = \lambda f.\, (\lambda x.\, f\,(x\,x))\,(\lambda x.\, f\,(x\,x)).
\]
$Y\,g$ head reduces to $g\,(Y\,g)$, so a pure term can denote a recursive, hence infinite, B\"ohm
tree, as the cyclic stream and the $\mathtt{map}$ output below do.

A list is Scott encoded: it is a function that, given a $\mathtt{cons}$ case $c$ and a $\mathtt{nil}$
case $n$, selects the matching one.
\[
  \mathtt{cons} = \lambda h.\,\lambda t.\,\lambda c.\,\lambda n.\, c\,h\,t, \qquad
  \mathtt{nil} = \lambda c.\,\lambda n.\, n, \qquad
  \mathtt{present} = \lambda a.\,\lambda b.\, a.
\]
A cell $\mathtt{cons}\,h\,t$ hands its head $h$ and tail $t$ to $c$, while $\mathtt{nil}$ returns $n$;
$\mathtt{present}$ is the constant the walk below returns at a non-empty cell. Numerals are Church
encoded, with $0$ and the successor:
\[
  0 = \lambda s.\,\lambda z.\, z, \qquad
  \mathtt{succ} = \lambda n.\,\lambda s.\,\lambda z.\, s\,(n\,s\,z).
\]

The first example is the canonical diverging term, and the pure rendering of $\mathtt{letrec}\;x=x$:
\[
  \Omega = \omega\,\omega = (\lambda x.\,x\,x)(\lambda x.\,x\,x), \qquad \mathtt{loop} = Y\,I.
\]
Neither has a head normal form, so its B\"ohm tree is the meaningless $\bot$. Head normalization
searches for that head normal form forever; $\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$ reaches the same
$\bot$ in finite time, the unproductive cycle.

The second example is the cyclic datum, the stream of zeros written purely with $Y$:
\[
  r = Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\,0), \qquad\text{denoting } r = \mathtt{cons}\,0\,r.
\]
The B\"ohm tree of $r$ is the infinite stream $\mathtt{cons}\,0\,(\mathtt{cons}\,0\,(\cdots))$. Head
normalization enumerates it by unfolding and never finishes; $\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$ is
the same tree, folded to a finite cyclic cell, the node $c$ with $c = \mathtt{cons}\,0\,c$.

The third example is an ordinary recursive $\mathtt{map}$, with nothing cycle-aware in it:
\[
  \mathtt{map} = \lambda f.\, Y\,\big(\lambda \mathit{self}.\,\lambda \mathit{xs}.\,
       \mathit{xs}\,(\lambda h.\,\lambda t.\, \mathtt{cons}\,(f\,h)\,(\mathit{self}\,t))\,\mathtt{nil}\big).
\]
Its recursive call sits under a $\mathtt{cons}$, so it is guarded. The map of interest increments
every element of $r$,
\[
  \mathtt{map}\;\mathtt{succ}\;r,
\]
whose B\"ohm tree is the infinite stream $\mathtt{cons}\,1\,(\mathtt{cons}\,1\,(\cdots))$, since
$\mathtt{succ}\,0 = 1$. Head normalization maps the infinite stream forever;
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$ is the same tree, folded to the finite cyclic cell $c'$ with
$c' = \mathtt{cons}\,1\,c'$.

The unguarded companion is the naive walk, whose recursive call exposes no constructor:
\[
  \mathtt{reach} = Y\,\big(\lambda \mathit{self}.\,\lambda \mathit{xs}.\,
       \mathit{xs}\,(\lambda h.\,\lambda t.\, \mathit{self}\,t)\,\mathtt{present}\big).
\]
Applied to $r$ it never reaches $\mathtt{nil}$, so $\mathtt{reach}\,r$ has no head normal form and its
B\"ohm tree is $\bot$. Head normalization builds a fresh recursive call at every step and loops;
$\operatorname{lfp}(\out_{\mathrm h})$ returns $\bot$ in finite time.
