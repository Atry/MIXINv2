\begin{abstract}
A $\lambda$-term's Berarducci tree is the readout of a single first-order
relation: the weak-head \emph{shape} relation $\Sh$ over the positions of the
term. The shape at a position is single-valued, so $\Sh$ has two resolutions that
differ at exactly one place. Resolved operationally by head reduction it
\emph{is} the Berarducci tree; resolved by \emph{stabilization}, re-evaluating
each position until its value stops changing in the manner of an AngularJS digest,
it conservatively extends the tree. The two differ only at an unproductive head
cycle, resolved as divergence ($\bot$) operationally and as a stabilized empty
value ($\emptyset$) under the digest. Such cycles occur only in genuinely cyclic
(shared) terms, never in pure $\lambda$-terms, so the stabilizing reading agrees
with the Berarducci tree on the whole pure-$\lambda$ fragment and adds meaning only
to cyclic structures. Because positions are interned, so that structural equality
is pointer equality, a guarded cyclic datum folds into a finite rational tree, and
structural computation over it terminates where head reduction diverges.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
The $\lambda$-calculus is the higher-order calculus, yet its tree semantics
factors through a \emph{first-order} relation. We make this precise: the
Berarducci tree $\BeT(M)$ of a term $M$ is the coinductive readout of a single
relation $\Sh$, the \emph{weak-head shape} of a position, which is first-order
in that it ranges over positions (graph nodes) and never over a function space.

Once the tree is presented this way, two observations become immediate. First,
$\Sh$ is a definite (Horn) logic program. Second, a definite program has two
standard meanings---an operational one (head reduction / SLD resolution) and a
declarative one (the least Herbrand model)---and these meanings come apart only
on atoms whose sole support is an unproductive cycle. The operational reading of
$\Sh$ is the Berarducci tree. The least-model reading is a value-recursion
semantics: it gives a defined (empty) value where the operational reading
diverges, and it terminates on cyclic data where head reduction unfolds forever.

These two meanings are not separate constructions but one knob. The shape at a
position is single-valued (a deterministic calculus exposes one weak-head
constructor), so there is no set to aggregate: the digest stabilizes each
position, and an unproductive head cycle stabilizes at an empty value. The only
free decision is how that empty value reads out. Sending it to $\bot$ gives the
operational reading; sending it to the term $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, the second
Scott constructor, gives the stabilizing reading. The whole paper is this single
choice $\emptyset \mapsto \{\bot,\ \lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b\}$: the two readings
agree elsewhere, conservativity and full abstraction track it, and the first
reading is the one for which Berarducci-tree equivalence is fully abstract.

The reason any of this is effective is the word in the title. Stabilization must
recognise when it has returned to a position it is already computing, both to fold
a cycle and to memoise, and recognising a repeated position is a test of
\emph{identity}. On a first-order domain identity is equality of positions, a
comparison of finite paths decidable by a pointer test, and \emph{interning} the
positions makes that pointer test exact; on the higher-order values of the
$\lambda$-calculus identity is $\beta$-equality, which is undecidable. Reading the
tree off a first-order relation is precisely what makes memoisation and the folding
of cyclic structures computable; the higher-order view affords neither.
``First-order'' is not a description of $\Sh$ but the mechanism that the results
rest on.

We never normalise to an intermediate form; in particular we do not use A-normal
form. Positions are read directly off the term, and the dynamics live entirely in
$\Sh$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A presentation of the Berarducci tree as the readout of a first-order
        shape relation $\Sh$ (Sections~\ref{sec:firstorder}, \ref{sec:grounding}).
  \item Two resolutions of $\Sh$: the operational reading $\Sbot$, which we show
        equals the Berarducci tree, and the stabilizing reading $\Semp$, an
        iterate-until-stable digest in the manner of AngularJS
        (Sections~\ref{sec:tworeadings}, \ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:valrec}).
  \item A bridge theorem: $\Sbot$ and $\Semp$ differ only at unproductive head
        cycles, which are absent from pure $\lambda$-terms; hence $\Semp$ is a
        conservative extension of $\BeT$ (Section~\ref{sec:bridge}). Together with
        the previous item this part is light: once $\Sh$ is given its positive
        presentation (Remark~\ref{rem:positivity}), well-definedness of the digest's
        limit and the locus of difference are standard least-fixpoint facts.
  \item As an application, structural computation over cyclic data
        (Section~\ref{sec:application}). Interning the positions folds a guarded
        cyclic datum into a finite rational tree, so a property over it, computed by
        the same stabilizing digest and keyed on node identity, terminates where
        head reduction diverges; $\Omega$, having no shared cell, is untouched and
        stays $\bot$. This is intrusive in that a new property reconstructs the
        datum; the non-intrusive, composable form is left to future work
        (Section~\ref{sec:future}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Overview}\label{sec:overview}
% =====================================================================
Consider a cyclic stream of zeros, written as the cyclic equation
$r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, and contrast it with the pure $\lambda$-term
$Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$ that has the \emph{same} Berarducci tree (the infinite
stream $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$). The two denote the same
tree but are different objects: the first is a cyclic graph (a shared back-edge),
the second is acyclic and unfolds by copying.

Head reduction enumerates this tree the same way in both cases---and never
finishes. The least-model reading behaves differently on the two: on the cyclic
graph it folds the back-edge (the node's identity is a visited set, for free) and
returns a finite cyclic representation, so a structural query such as ``does $r$
reach itself'' terminates; on the acyclic $Y$-term there is no back-edge to fold,
so it unfolds forever, exactly like head reduction.

The whole difference between the two readings is one knob: how $\Sh$ resolves an
\emph{unproductive} cycle---a cycle that returns to a node before exposing any
constructor. Operationally this is divergence ($\bot$); in the least model it is
the empty set ($\emptyset$). Pure $\lambda$-terms never present such a cycle, so
the readings coincide on all of them.

\section{Berarducci trees as the readout of a first-order shape relation}
\label{sec:firstorder}

\subsection{Terms as graphs}
We work with $\lambda$-term graphs. A node is labelled $\Lamsh$ (an abstraction,
with an edge to its body), $\Appsh$ (an application, with edges to function and
argument), or $\Varsh$ (a variable; we use de Bruijn indices to avoid
$\alpha$-renaming). Edges may point to any node, including ancestors; an edge to
an ancestor is a \emph{back-edge} and makes the graph cyclic. Pure
$\lambda$-terms are exactly the \emph{acyclic} graphs (trees, or DAGs under
sharing); cyclic graphs arise only from recursive binding (e.g.\ \texttt{letrec}).
$\beta$-substitution is by reference: substituting an argument preserves the
argument's node identity (sharing); only the copied redex body is fresh.

\subsection{The shape relation}
\begin{definition}[Weak-head shape]\label{def:sh}
The relation $\Sh$ assigns to a node its outermost constructor after weak head
reduction:
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Sh(\,\mathit{body}[x \mathbin{:=} a]\,)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,\mathit{body}) && \text{(head redex)}\\
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Appsh(f,a)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) \text{ is rigid} && \text{(root-stable)}\\
  \Sh(\Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)) &= \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N) && &&\\
  \Sh(\Varsh(x)) &= \Varsh(x). && &&
\end{aligned}
\]
A node is \emph{rigid} when its head is a variable or is itself root-stable
(non-abstraction). $\Sh$ performs \emph{weak} head reduction only: it stops at the
outermost constructor and does not reduce under $\lambda$ or inside arguments.
\end{definition}

\noindent
$\Sh$ is first-order: it is a relation over nodes, with no quantification over a
function space.

\begin{remark}[Positivity]\label{rem:positivity}
The clauses of $\Sh$ are \emph{definite}: every premise is an atom $\Sh(\cdot)=c$,
and there is no negation. In particular ``rigid'' must be read as the positive
disjunction ``head is a $\Varsh$, or head is itself a root-stable $\Appsh$'', never
as the negative ``head is not a $\Lamsh$''; a negation-as-failure reading would
break positivity and forfeit the least-fixpoint semantics below. Positivity makes
the immediate-consequence operator $T_{\Sh}$ monotone (indeed continuous), so by
Knaster--Tarski its least fixpoint exists and is unique, and the least Herbrand
model $\Semp$ of Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings} is well-defined and sound by
construction \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}. Soundness of $\Semp$ therefore costs
nothing beyond fixing this positive presentation; the work of the paper is
elsewhere (Sections~\ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:application}).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Positions are Berarducci-tree addresses}
\begin{definition}[Path]\label{def:path}
The descent alphabet is $\Delta = \{\dlam,\dhead,\darg\}$. A \emph{path} is a word
in $\Delta^{*}$. The valid paths of $M$ and the subterm $\subt{M}{p}$ at a valid
path $p$ are defined coinductively together with $\Sh$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\varepsilon$ is valid and $\subt{M}{\varepsilon} = M$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)$ then $p\,\dlam$ is valid and
        $\subt{M}{p\,\dlam} = N$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(R,Q)$ then $p\,\dhead$ and $p\,\darg$ are
        valid, $\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}=R$ and $\subt{M}{p\,\darg}=Q$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)$ then $p$ is a leaf;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p})$ is undefined then $p$ is a leaf.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\noindent
A path is purely a static address into the tree being built; all dynamics (which
positions exist, where divergence occurs) are delegated to $\Sh$.

\begin{definition}[Tree readout]\label{def:btree}
\[
\Btree(\subt{M}{p}) =
\begin{cases}
  x & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)\\
  \lambda.\;\Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dlam}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\cdots)\\
  \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}) \cdot \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\darg}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(\cdots)\\
  \bot & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) \text{ undefined.}
\end{cases}
\]
The application case is retained even when the head subtree is $\bot$
(a $\bot$-headed application node is kept, not collapsed).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Definitions~\ref{def:sh}--\ref{def:btree} are the Berarducci tree
\citeTODO{berarducci}, re-presented as the readout of $\Sh$. Two design points are
load-bearing: $\Sh$ observes a \emph{weak} head normal form (it stops at the
outermost $\lambda$), not a head normal form; and the readout \emph{retains}
$\bot$-headed applications. The first places the construction in the
Berarducci/L\'evy--Longo family rather than the B\"ohm family
\citeTODO{barendregt, levy-longo}; the second pins it to Berarducci specifically.
\end{remark}

\section{Two resolutions of the shape relation}\label{sec:tworeadings}
% =====================================================================
$\Sh$ in Definition~\ref{def:sh} assigns each position a \emph{single} shape: a
deterministic calculus exposes one weak-head constructor, so there is no set of
shapes to collect and nothing to aggregate. $\Sh$ has two resolutions, and the
difference between them is the only knob in this paper.

\begin{definition}[Operational reading $\Sbot$]
$\Sbot$ resolves $\Sh$ by head reduction. When computing $\Sh(n)$ returns to $n$
before exposing a constructor (an unproductive head cycle), the reduction loops;
we read this as $\Sh(n)$ undefined, i.e.\ $\bot$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Stabilizing reading $\Semp$]
$\Semp$ resolves $\Sh$ by \emph{stabilization}: each position is re-evaluated, from
the bottom value upward, until its shape stops changing, in the manner of an
AngularJS digest (re-run the recurrence to a fixed point, bounded by a
time-to-live). Because positions are interned, a return to a position already under
computation is caught by a pointer test, so an unproductive head cycle stabilizes
at the empty value $\emptyset$ (no constructor ever exposed). This limit is the
least fixpoint of the shape recurrence \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}; computing it by
iterate-until-stable, rather than as a set-valued least model, is what keeps it
effective when a position could otherwise be reached in unboundedly many ways.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Locus of difference]\label{prop:locus}
$\Sbot$ and $\Semp$ agree on every node whose shape is either derivable in
finitely many steps or undefined for a reason other than an unproductive head
cycle. They differ exactly on nodes with an unproductive head cycle, where
$\Sbot$ gives $\bot$ and $\Semp$ gives $\emptyset$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
This is the classical gap between SLD termination and least-model membership for
definite programs \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}: an atom is in the least model iff
it has a finite derivation; an unproductive cycle has no finite derivation
(least-model value $\emptyset$) but the unmemoised operational search loops
($\bot$). \TODO{state and prove precisely, fixing the notion of ``unproductive
head cycle''.}
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Codomains]\label{rem:codomains}
$\Sbot$ takes values in Berarducci trees with $\bot$. $\Semp$ takes values in
Berarducci trees with \emph{two} bottom-like leaves, $\bot$ and $\emptyset$,
which are distinct: $\bot$ is ``undefined / nonterminating'', $\emptyset$ is
``defined, but the empty set of shapes''. The extra leaf $\emptyset$ is precisely
where $\Semp$ exceeds $\BeT$.
\end{remark}

\section{Grounding: \texorpdfstring{$\Sbot = \BeT$}{S\_bot = BeT}}
\label{sec:grounding}
% =====================================================================
\begin{theorem}[Presentation adequacy]\label{thm:adequacy}
For every term $M$, $\Btree(\subt{M}{\varepsilon}) = \BeT(M)$ under the
operational reading $\Sbot$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The operational $\Sh$ is weak head reduction; the three constructor cases of
Definition~\ref{def:btree} are the three cases of the Berarducci tree
construction; retaining $\bot$-headed applications matches Berarducci's treatment
of root-stable applications with a meaningless head; and an undefined $\Sh$
(looping head reduction) is exactly a root-active term, i.e.\ a $\bot$ leaf. The
result follows by coinduction on the tree. \TODO{write out the coinductive
argument; verify the root-active $\Leftrightarrow$ undefined-$\Sh$ correspondence
against Berarducci's definition \citeTODO{berarducci}.}
\end{proof}

\noindent
Because we work on raw terms, there is no normal-form translation to justify and
no administrative-redex bridge: $\Sbot = \BeT$ holds essentially by construction,
and the only content is that Definitions~\ref{def:path}--\ref{def:btree} present
weak head reduction faithfully.

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\Semp$}{S\_empty}: least Herbrand model and value-recursion}
\label{sec:valrec}
% =====================================================================
$\Semp$ is the least Herbrand model of $\Sh$. The only nodes on which it can
differ from $\Sbot$ are those reached through a back-edge, and the behaviour
splits on whether the cycle is productive.

\begin{definition}[Guarded vs.\ unproductive cycle]
A back-edge to a node $n$ is \emph{guarded} if $\Sh(n)$ is determined (a
constructor is exposed) before the back-edge is reached; it is \emph{unproductive}
if computing $\Sh(n)$ returns to $n$ with no constructor exposed.
\end{definition}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Guarded.} $\Sh(n)$ is a constructor and the back-edge sits in a
        child position. The readout folds at the back-edge, yielding a
        \emph{rational} (regular) infinite tree, finitely represented by the
        cycle. This is the \emph{same} tree as $\Sbot$ would unfold; $\Semp$
        differs only in computing a finite representation (and terminating).
  \item \textbf{Unproductive.} $\Sh(n)$ has only base-less self-support; its least
        solution is $\emptyset$. Here $\Semp(n) = \emptyset$ whereas
        $\Sbot(n) = \bot$. This is value-recursion.
\end{itemize}

\begin{example}[Guarded: $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$]\label{ex:cons}
With the Scott encoding $\mathtt{cons} = \lambda h.\lambda t.\lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,h\,t$,
\[
  \Sh(r) = \Sh\big((\lambda t.\lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,0\,t)\;r\big)
         = \Sh\big(\lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,0\,r\big) = \Lamsh,
\]
derived in finitely many steps without ever requiring $\Sh(r)$ again: $r$ enters
only as data, never as a head. So $\Sh(r)$ is productive and the two readings
agree at this level. The readout under $\Semp$ folds at the (guarded) back-edge:
\[
  \rho \;=\; \lambda c.\,\lambda n.\,(c\cdot 0)\cdot \rho,
\]
the finite cyclic representation of the rational tree
$\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\cdots))$.
$\Sbot(r)$ is the same infinite tree but has no finite representation, so its
enumeration diverges.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Unproductive: $\mathtt{letrec}\;x = x\;\mathtt{in}\;x$]\label{ex:xx}
The node $x$ is a back-edge to itself with no intervening constructor, so the only
clause is $\Sh(x) = \Sh(x)$, with no base case. Under $\Sbot$, head reduction
chases $x\to x\to\cdots$ and loops: $\Sbot(x) = \bot$. Under $\Semp$, the least
fixpoint of a base-less self-supporting clause is empty: $\Semp(x) = \emptyset$.
The two readings differ here; this is the defining case of value-recursion.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Copy: $\Omega$ and $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$]\label{ex:copy}
$\Omega = (\lambda x.\,x\,x)(\lambda x.\,x\,x)$ is acyclic; head reduction never
exposes a constructor and produces ever-fresh nodes, so both readings give $\bot$.
$Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$ is also acyclic and has the same tree as
Example~\ref{ex:cons}, but its recursion unfolds by copying (fresh nodes, no
back-edge), so $\Semp$ has nothing to fold and diverges exactly as $\Sbot$ does.
In both pure-$\lambda$ cases the readings coincide.
\end{example}

\section{Bridge: conservativity over the Berarducci tree}\label{sec:bridge}
% =====================================================================
\begin{lemma}[Acyclicity]\label{lem:acyclic}
A pure $\lambda$-term is an acyclic graph, and $\beta$-reduction does not introduce
a back-edge. Consequently no pure $\lambda$-term presents an unproductive head
cycle.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Cyclic graphs correspond exactly to recursive binding; pure $\lambda$-terms have
none, and graph-$\beta$ rewrites an acyclic graph to an acyclic graph (the
contractum reuses the shared argument but creates only fresh internal structure).
\TODO{make precise and cite the cyclic-graph / explicit-recursion correspondence
\citeTODO{ariola-klop}; this is the one substantive structural lemma of the paper.}
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bridge / conservativity]\label{thm:bridge}
On every pure $\lambda$-term, $\Semp = \Sbot = \BeT$. Hence $\Semp$ is a
conservative extension of the Berarducci tree: it agrees with $\BeT$ on the pure
fragment and adds new values ($\emptyset$, and finite representations of rational
trees) only on genuinely cyclic graphs.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:acyclic} pure $\lambda$-terms have no unproductive head cycle, so
by Proposition~\ref{prop:locus} $\Semp = \Sbot$ on them; by
Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy} $\Sbot = \BeT$.
\end{proof}

\noindent
The slogan is \emph{copy vs.\ share}: copying (pure $\lambda$) yields agreement of
the two readings; sharing (a back-edge) is the only thing that makes $\Semp$
either fold a rational tree or return $\emptyset$.

\section{Applications}
\label{sec:application}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{Reading the empty value back into the calculus}
\label{sec:collector}
Stabilization (Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings}) gives each position a single value: a
shape, or $\emptyset$ at an unproductive cycle. To consume that inside the
$\lambda$-calculus the only choice is how $\emptyset$ reads out; a present shape is
forced to read out as itself. We encode $\emptyset$ as a first-class value, so that
emptiness is case-analysable with no external $\mathtt{isNil}$ primitive, while a
present value keeps its wrapper-free readout and so stays conservative over $\BeT$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}).

\paragraph{$\emptyset$ is a distinct leaf, and why the digest is sound}
Semantically $\emptyset$ is its own leaf, distinct from $\bot$ and from every
$\lambda$-term (Remark~\ref{rem:codomains}). This distinctness is exactly what makes
the stabilizing digest sound: its bottom is this fresh $\emptyset$, not a reused
value, so a position still sitting at $\emptyset$ is never mistaken for one that has
resolved. Were the bottom instead a genuine value such as the second Scott
constructor $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, the stability test would become ambiguous (a
position at the placeholder is indistinguishable from one that truly computed
$\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$) and a round could even read a dependency's placeholder
as that real value and stabilize a \emph{wrong} fixed point. So $\Semp$ keeps
$\emptyset$ as its own leaf, and the readout of a present shape is the value itself.

\paragraph{An optional object-level encoding, under a side condition}
To consume absence \emph{within} the calculus one may encode $\emptyset$ as
$\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, so that the case analysis $w\;a\;b$ on a readout $w$
selects $b$ exactly when $w$ is absent, with a present value occupying the first
constructor ($\present = \lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,a$) and left unwrapped. This is the
type-safe comma-ok idiom, but it is sound \emph{only} under a side condition: the
term's present values must avoid the slot $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, for otherwise
a genuine $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ and an empty cycle would be conflated and the
false branch would fire for both. A query whose present values are a single marker
$\present$ (reachability, Section~\ref{sec:reach}) satisfies it; in general
$\emptyset$ is kept as the distinct leaf above. Either way there is no aggregate and
no deduplication: the shape is single-valued, and the only test of identity, the one
that lets the digest fold a cycle, is a pointer comparison of interned positions,
not the undecidable $\beta$-equality of values.

\paragraph{Conservativity is preserved}
Because a present shape reads out as $v$ itself, with no wrapper, a pure
$\lambda$-term that converges to $v$ still reads out to $v$, so $\Semp = \BeT$
there (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}).
The empty readout $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ appears only where $\Semp$ already
differs from $\BeT$, namely at an unproductive cycle (Remark~\ref{rem:codomains});
giving that extra leaf this object-level body chooses a representation for it, it
does not introduce a new disagreement on the convergent fragment.

\paragraph{The comma-ok idiom}
The encoding is the type-safe form of the multiple-return convention of Go
($v,\mathit{ok} := m[k]$) and Lua: absence is signalled in a secondary channel
while the value itself is returned unwrapped. The difference is that a Scott case
is a sum, not a product: when the result is $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ there is no
value at all, so the consumer is forced to handle the absent branch rather than
silently reading a leftover zero value.

\paragraph{The knob, and full abstraction}\label{sec:variants}
The empty readout $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ and the operational $\bot$ are the two
values the empty cycle may take, and they are the whole knob: the readings agree on
every present shape (each reads out as itself) and part only at $\emptyset$. The
choice $\emptyset = \bot$ is the one that lands on a classical tree equivalence.
Under it, by Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy} the denotation of a term is its Berarducci
tree, and observation through the readout---place a term in a closing context and
watch whether the readout converges, exposing a head constructor---identifies two
terms exactly when their Berarducci trees coincide. Berarducci-tree equivalence is
thus \emph{fully abstract} for $\emptyset = \bot$: contextual equivalence equals tree
equality. Soundness is the easy direction; the content is completeness, that a
difference in the trees surfaces at some finite path and a context can drive the
readout to it. \TODO{state the contextual-equivalence relation precisely and prove
both directions; cite the B\"ohm-out technique adapted to the Berarducci setting.}

The stabilizing reading is strictly finer. Taking $\emptyset = \lambda a.\,\lambda
b.\,b$ tells an unproductive cycle (a value a context can case-split on) apart from
genuine divergence ($\bot$), so it distinguishes terms whose Berarducci trees agree;
hence $\Semp$ is \emph{not} fully abstract for Berarducci-tree equivalence. Full
abstraction is exactly the property $\emptyset = \bot$ has and $\emptyset = \lambda
a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ gives up, in exchange for the extra definedness of
Section~\ref{sec:application}. Both readings are conservative over $\BeT$ for the
same reason: a present shape reads out unwrapped, and a pure $\lambda$-term has no
unproductive cycle (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}), so $\emptyset$ never arises there and
the choice is invisible on the convergent fragment.

\subsection{Terminating recursive queries over cyclic data}
\label{sec:reach}
Under $\Semp$ a node's identity acts as a visited set: revisiting it is a
back-edge, detected for free. Structural queries over Scott-encoded cyclic data
that recurse along the structure---reachability, transitive closure, membership
under a cyclic representation---therefore terminate, whereas head reduction
unfolds the cycle and diverges.

Reachability is the clean instance, and it sidesteps the obstacle of
Remark~\ref{rem:identity}. The query returns a boolean, so the values collected
at any position are drawn from $\{\present\}$ together with the empty collection:
a reachable witness contributes $\present$, an exhausted search contributes
nothing. By the aggregate function of Section~\ref{sec:collector}, a reachable position
reads out as $\mathrm{agg}\,\{\present,\ldots,\present\} = \present$ and an unreachable
one as $\mathrm{agg}\,\emptyset = \lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$. Because all collected
witnesses are the single value $\present$, the ``else diverge'' case never fires,
so reachability stays within the well-defined fragment. Termination does not
rest on giving each recursive call a distinct identity; it rests on the value
domain being a single positive value $\present$, deduplicated by first-order
position and accumulated up a finite ascending chain. The fresh application nodes
built by the recursion proliferate, but their \emph{values} all collapse to one
$\present$, so the query converges where head reduction unfolds forever. The
consumer reads the answer with a single Scott case, with no external
$\mathtt{isNil}$: the unreachable result $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ already
selects the false branch. \TODO{give the concrete $\mathtt{reach}$ program over a Scott-encoded
cyclic graph and the explicit $\Semp$ derivation; state the termination criterion
(finite reachable set / rational denotation).}

\begin{remark}[The identity criterion is the crux]\label{rem:identity}
There is a genuine obstacle hidden in the previous paragraph. A query such as
$\mathtt{reach}$ recurses by building \emph{fresh} application nodes (only its
data argument is shared), so under the node-identity reading that grounds
Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge} its shape atoms are all distinct and it diverges exactly
as $\Sbot$ does. Terminating it requires keying the table on the data argument's
\emph{value} rather than on application-node identity. But value keying is the
reading that makes $\Omega = (\lambda x.x\,x)(\lambda x.x\,x)$ a cycle---its
contractum is content-identical to itself---so it assigns $\Omega = \emptyset$,
breaking the agreement $\Semp = \BeT$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}. The two
identity criteria are in direct conflict: node identity grounds the tree but
diverges queries; value identity terminates queries but is not conservative.
Reconciling them---node identity for the reduction relation $\Sh$ (which also
renders cyclic data \emph{rational}, i.e.\ with finitely many distinct subtrees),
and value/subtree keying for queries over that rational denotation---is the
substantive theorem this application rests on. \TODO{state and prove the two-level
reconciliation, including that the query layer does not leak back into $\Sh$ and
revive $\Omega = \emptyset$.}
\end{remark}

\subsection{Memoising arbitrary programs}
\label{sec:memo}
More generally, $\Semp$ is the denotation of a \emph{memoised} run of any
$\lambda$-program. Memoisation needs a comparable key on which to recognise a
repeated call; the higher-order view offers none, since value equality of
functions is undecidable. The first-order reading replaces values by positions,
whose identity is comparable (a pointer test, or a first-order shape), so it is
the natural setting in which memoisation is even definable.

On terminating computations memoisation is transparent (referential
transparency). Its only semantic effect is on computations that loop by revisiting
a pending call: there it returns the least-fixpoint value rather than diverging,
converging $\bot$ to $\emptyset$. The aggressiveness of memoisation is precisely
the choice of identity criterion, and it is the knob of
Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings} generalised:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{no memoisation} ($\Sbot = \BeT$);
  \item \emph{node-identity memoisation} (conservative over $\BeT$ by
        Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}; fires only on explicit sharing);
  \item \emph{value/content memoisation} (terminates any value-cycle, including
        queries such as reachability, but is no longer conservative---$\Omega$
        becomes a value-cycle, assigned $\emptyset$ rather than $\bot$).
\end{itemize}
The trade-off is monotone: more aggressive memoisation terminates more programs
and identifies more, eventually giving up agreement with $\BeT$. Effectiveness
follows the same gradient---value keying is computable only where value equality
is decidable (first-order / rational results), whereas node keying is always
computable but catches only explicit sharing. \TODO{make the spectrum precise:

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}
% =====================================================================
\paragraph{Trees and infinitary $\lambda$-calculus.}
The Berarducci, L\'evy--Longo, and B\"ohm trees form a hierarchy by their
meaningless sets \citeTODO{berarducci, levy-longo, barendregt}; the infinitary
$\lambda$-calculus framework \citeTODO{kennaway-klop-sleep-devries} situates them
uniformly. Our $\Sbot$ is the Berarducci tree (weak-head observation, retained
$\bot$-headed applications).

\paragraph{Least Herbrand models and tabling.}
$\Semp$ is the least Herbrand model of a definite program
\citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}; the difference from $\Sbot$ is the SLD-termination /
least-model gap, and a tabled evaluator \citeTODO{tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}
computes $\Semp$ by completing unproductive cycles instead of looping. Tabling is
the canonical mechanism for terminating left-recursive and cyclic fixpoint queries
that plain resolution diverges on; our query application
(Section~\ref{sec:reach}) is an instance of it transported into the
$\lambda$-calculus.

\paragraph{Rational trees and recursive types.}
The capability of deciding structural queries over cyclic structures by
cycle detection is long established outside the $\lambda$-calculus. Rational
(regular) infinite trees and their algebra \citeTODO{courcelle} underlie cyclic
unification in logic programming \citeTODO{colmerauer}, where the occurs-check is
dropped and cyclic terms are first class. In type theory, equirecursive
(\emph{regular}) recursive types give terminating algorithms for subtyping and
equality precisely by detecting cycles in the type's regular tree
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. These are the same phenomenon as our
guarded-cycle case---a rational denotation with finitely many distinct
subtrees---reached by different routes. The point of difference is one of
position rather than capability: those approaches add machinery (a unification
discipline, a separate type-level system), whereas $\Semp$ is a conservative
re-reading of the $\lambda$-calculus's own tree semantics
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}); and the node-identity-versus-value-identity collision
of Remark~\ref{rem:identity} is specific to a setting with $\beta$-reduction,
which those first-order settings do not have.

\paragraph{Cyclic $\lambda$-terms and recursion.}
Cyclic graphs / explicit recursion \citeTODO{ariola-klop} and the call-by-need
$\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{ariola-felleisen} provide the sharing on which the
$\Semp$/$\Sbot$ difference rests; the guarded-cycle case yields rational trees,
and the productivity distinction is the standard one from infinitary rewriting.

\paragraph{Sharing in functional languages.}
That a pure functional language cannot observe sharing or cyclicity---our
copy-versus-share dichotomy---is a known obstacle: traversals of a cyclic
structure diverge because referential transparency hides the back-edge. Existing
remedies \emph{add} something to the language, such as observable sharing
\citeTODO{gill} or the deliberate use of laziness in circular programs
\citeTODO{bird}. We add nothing: the sharing is read off the graph and given
meaning by $\Semp$.

\paragraph{Higher-order model checking.}
Deciding properties of the (possibly infinite) trees generated by higher-order
programs \citeTODO{ong-hors, kobayashi} shares the goal of answering queries about
$\lambda$-generated infinite trees, but proceeds by automata and games over
B\"ohm-tree-like denotations rather than by a least-model reading of a shape
relation.

\paragraph{Lazy $\lambda$-calculus.}
The lazy $\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{abramsky-ong} observes weak head normal
forms but, unlike the Berarducci reading, does not retain $\bot$-headed
applications; its tree is L\'evy--Longo, not Berarducci.

\paragraph{Defunctionalization.}
Unlike defunctionalization \citeTODO{reynolds}, which compiles a higher-order
program into a first-order interpreter, we give a first-order semantic
\emph{object} for the higher-order calculus; no interpreter is constructed.

\paragraph{Position.}
The capability we exploit---terminating structural computation over cyclic
data by cycle detection---is decades old in logic programming
\citeTODO{colmerauer, tamaki-sato} and in recursive-type theory
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. What appears to be new is its
position: it is obtained as a conservative reading of the $\lambda$-calculus's own
Berarducci-tree semantics, without leaving the calculus or adding primitives, and
the obstacle that makes this non-trivial---reconciling the node identity required
for the tree with the value identity required for queries
(Remark~\ref{rem:identity})---arises only because the $\lambda$-calculus has both
$\beta$-reduction and cyclic data, a combination the prior first-order settings do
not face.

% =====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =====================================================================
The Berarducci tree is the readout of a first-order shape relation; reading that
relation as a definite logic program gives, operationally, the tree itself, and,
in the least Herbrand model, a value-recursion semantics that conservatively
extends it. The two coincide except at unproductive head cycles, which pure
$\lambda$-terms cannot present. The mechanism is not specific to the
$\lambda$-calculus and extends to richer binding and data structures.
