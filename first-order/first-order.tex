\begin{abstract}
The pure $\lambda$-calculus is commonly held to be unable to manipulate cyclic data without extending it with a recursion construct such as \texttt{letrec} or a $\mu$-binder; we demonstrate that no such extension is needed. The behaviour of a coalgebra, like HNF, can be read as a finite cyclic graph. A state is identified with its own unfolding, so a behaviour with finitely many distinct subtrees, a \emph{rational} behaviour, collapses to a finite graph closed by back edges, and \emph{tabling} computes it: a state revisited during its own readout ties a cycle instead of unfolding forever. The structure map need not be given outright; it may itself be the least fixpoint of an iterative process, and the ascent from $\bot$ makes a silent cycle terminate as $\bot$ in finite time where the process alone diverges. Head normalisation of the pure $\lambda$-calculus is one instance, and the one that bears out the opening claim: with weak-head reduction as the iterative process, $\Omega$ terminates as $\bot$ and pure $\lambda$-terms converge to cyclic graphs, with no construct added to the calculus. The construction needs only that observations have a computable rational fixpoint and that internal steps can be iterated. Cycles fold by the ordinary behaviour of tabling, on the identity of states, which is decidable because the states are \emph{first-order}, ranging over graph nodes and never over a function space. Deterministic reduction strategies, head and weak-head normalisation among them, are the special case where the iterated effect is mere divergence.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
The $\lambda$-calculus is the higher-order calculus, yet its tree semantics
factors through a \emph{first-order} relation. We make this precise for any tree presented by a
monotone first-order head-evaluation (Section~\ref{sec:firstorder}): the tree
$\lfpT(M)$ of a term $M$ is the coinductive readout of a single
relation $\Sh$, the \emph{shape} of a position, which is first-order
in that it ranges over positions (graph nodes) and never over a function space. The weak-head
shape we use throughout is one instance.

Once the tree is presented this way, one observation becomes immediate:
$\Sh$'s clauses are positive, so the immediate-consequence operator $T_P$ that reads
off the tree is monotone, and the tree is its least fixpoint $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$
(Knaster--Tarski). Because positions are taken up to a congruence, $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ folds a guarded cycle
into a finite rational graph, so it is an \emph{adequate monotone refinement} of head
reduction: it agrees with the tree head reduction enumerates, and additionally terminates where
head reduction diverges, deciding an unproductive cycle as $\bot$ in finite time.

A deterministic calculus exposes one constructor at a position. The value
at a position is therefore a \emph{partial}
tree, a tree with $\bot$ holes, and the least-fixpoint ascent fills the holes
until the tree stops growing. An unproductive cycle has nothing to fill
its hole, so it stays $\bot$. The bottom is $\bot$.

The reason any of this is effective is the word in the title. The merge must
recognise when it has returned to a position it is already computing, both to fold
a cycle and to memoise, and recognising a repeated position is a test of
\emph{identity}. That identity is a parameter, a \emph{position congruence}: on a
first-order domain it is decidable, the finest instance comparing finite de Bruijn
terms, a coarser one a richer but still decidable test; on the higher-order values
of the $\lambda$-calculus the only identity is $\beta$-equality, which is
undecidable. Reading the tree off a first-order relation is precisely what makes
identity a decidable, tunable parameter, and so makes memoisation and the folding of
cyclic structures computable; the higher-order view affords neither. ``First-order''
is not a description of $\Sh$ but the mechanism that the results rest on.

We never normalise to an intermediate form; in particular we do not use A-normal
form. Positions are read directly off the term, and the dynamics live entirely in
$\Sh$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A presentation of the tree of any monotone first-order head-evaluation as the readout of
        a first-order shape relation $\Sh$, parametric in the head-evaluation and in a sound
        \emph{position congruence} (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}), the weak-head shape and
        syntactic identity being the respective finest instances
        (Sections~\ref{sec:firstorder}, \ref{sec:grounding}). The congruence sits on a spectrum
        from syntactic identity to tree equality; soundness is the congruence law read
        coinductively (Remark~\ref{rem:soundness}), so it costs nothing to fix.
  \item $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is an \emph{adequate monotone refinement} of head reduction
        (Sections~\ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:valrec}): it is the tree
        (Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy}), so it agrees with the tree head reduction enumerates, and
        because positions are taken up to the congruence it folds a guarded cycle into the rational subtree and
        terminates where head reduction diverges, deciding an unproductive cycle as $\bot$ in
        finite time. Whether a readout terminates is governed by rationality
        (Theorem~\ref{thm:termination}): finitely many positions up to the congruence iff the tree
        is rational up to it. A coarser sound congruence folds more, up to a dead-subterm cycle the
        finest one cannot (Remark~\ref{rem:deadfold}). Once $\Sh$ is given its positive
        presentation (Remark~\ref{rem:positivity}), well-definedness of the least fixpoint is a
        standard fixpoint fact.
  \item As an application, structural computation over cyclic data
        (Section~\ref{sec:application}). Quotienting the positions folds a guarded
        cyclic datum into a finite rational tree, so a pure $\lambda$-term acquires a finite
        \emph{circular} representation the standard semantics cannot build, and an \emph{ordinary}
        recursive function over it terminates where head reduction diverges: the function's own
        recursive applications fall into the same classes and fold. An ordinary $\mathtt{map}$, with
        nothing cycle-aware in it, folds a circular list into a circular list.
\end{itemize}

both a rational and a non-rational stream.

\section{Overview}\label{sec:overview}
% =====================================================================
Consider a cyclic stream of zeros, written $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, and the pure
$\lambda$-term $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$, which has the \emph{same} tree
(the infinite stream $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$). Head reduction
enumerates this tree the same way in both cases, and never finishes.

The least fixpoint folds it. Once positions are taken up to the congruence, structurally equal
positions are one class, so the stream has finitely many distinct positions; the
least fixpoint folds the tail back onto the repeating cell, a node that points to
itself, a finite rational graph. Head reduction, which identifies nothing, enumerates the infinite
tree forever, so at this guarded cycle $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ terminates where head reduction does
not, while denoting the same tree.

At an \emph{unproductive} cycle, a position that returns to itself before exposing
any constructor, there is nothing to fold, so the least fixpoint settles at $\bot$. Such a
position is reached not only by a recursive binder ($\mathtt{letrec}\;x = x$) but by
self-application: $\Omega = (\lambda x.x\,x)(\lambda x.x\,x)$ returns to the same
position with no constructor exposed, so $\Omega = \bot$. Whether a readout terminates is
governed by rationality.

\section{Tree semantics as the readout of a first-order shape relation}
\label{sec:firstorder}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{Terms and the position congruence}\label{sec:congruence}
We work with pure $\lambda$-terms in de Bruijn form (avoiding $\alpha$-renaming);
a node is labelled $\Lamsh$, $\Appsh$, or $\Varsh$. A \emph{position} is a subterm
reached while computing a shape. Positions are identified up to a position congruence
$\approx$ (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}), the equality on which a re-entry is recognised and a
cycle folded. The choice of $\approx$ is the mechanism the results rest on: it makes the recurrence
for $\Sh$ a memoisable dynamic program keyed by the $\approx$-class of a position, and it makes that
class the finite key on which $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ folds a cycle. The finest instance is syntactic
(de Bruijn) identity, deciding a re-entry by equality of finite terms; a coarser sound congruence
(modulo a decidable theory, or up to dead subterms) folds strictly more, at the cost of a richer
identity test.
A term has finitely many positions up to $\approx$ when its
tree is \emph{rational} (finitely many distinct subtrees); for the finest congruence this can
fail, and a coarser sound congruence recovers it (Theorem~\ref{thm:termination}). A recursive
definition produces such repetition directly, and so does a self-application, whose contractum is
an existing position. No recursion binder belongs to the calculus:
the $Y$ combinator produces the repetition that the congruence folds
(Section~\ref{sec:valrec}).

\subsection{The tree domain}\label{sec:domain}
We fix the codomain of the readout, and the order along which the fixpoints below
ascend, before introducing the relation that drives them. The codomain is the usual
domain of finite-and-infinite trees with a bottom element; the order is information
refinement, replacing a $\bot$-hole by a more defined subtree.

\begin{definition}[Tree signature]\label{def:signature}
$\Sigma$ is the ranked alphabet with one binary symbol $\Appsh$, one unary symbol
$\Lamsh$, and the nullary symbols $\Varsh_i$ ($i \in \mathbb{N}$, de Bruijn indices)
and $\bot$. The descent alphabet $\Delta = \{\dlam,\dhead,\darg\}$
(Definition~\ref{def:path}) names argument positions: $\dlam$ under $\Lamsh$, and
$\dhead,\darg$ under $\Appsh$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Trees]\label{def:domain}
A \emph{tree} is a map $t \colon \operatorname{dom}(t) \to \Sigma$ whose
domain $\operatorname{dom}(t) \subseteq \Delta^{*}$ is prefix-closed and
arity-respecting: for each $p \in \operatorname{dom}(t)$ the children of $p$ in
$\operatorname{dom}(t)$ are exactly $\{p\,\dlam\}$ if $t(p)=\Lamsh$, $\{p\,\dhead,
p\,\darg\}$ if $t(p)=\Appsh$, and $\varnothing$ if $t(p) \in \{\Varsh_i,\bot\}$. Let
$D$ be the set of all such finite or infinite trees.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Approximation order]\label{def:order}
For $t,u \in D$, $t \sqsubseteq u$ iff $\operatorname{dom}(t) \subseteq
\operatorname{dom}(u)$ and for every $p \in \operatorname{dom}(t)$ either
$t(p)=u(p)$, or $t(p)=\bot$. Thus $u$ refines $t$ by replacing $\bot$-leaves with
subtrees.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[The codomain is a domain]\label{prop:cpo}
$(D,\sqsubseteq)$ is a pointed $\omega$-complete partial order with least element
$\bot_D = \{\varepsilon \mapsto \bot\}$, and least upper bounds of
$\sqsubseteq$-directed families exist and are computed nodewise. Proof in
Appendix~\ref{app:domain}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Rational tree]\label{def:rational}
$t \in D$ is \emph{rational} if its set of subtrees $\{\,t\!\restriction_{p} : p \in
\operatorname{dom}(t)\,\}$, where $t\!\restriction_{p}(q)=t(pq)$, is finite.
Equivalently, $t$ is the unfolding of a finite $\Sigma$-labelled graph.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Position set]\label{def:positions}
For a term $M \in \Lambda$, the \emph{position set} $\operatorname{Pos}(M) \subseteq
\Lambda$ is the least set of de Bruijn terms with $M \in \operatorname{Pos}(M)$ and,
for each $n \in \operatorname{Pos}(M)$: if $n=\Lamsh(\lambda x.N)$ then $N \in
\operatorname{Pos}(M)$; if $n=\Appsh(f,a)$ then $f,a \in \operatorname{Pos}(M)$; and
if $n=\Appsh(f,a)$ with $\Sh(f)=\Lamsh(\lambda x.\,\mathit{body})$ then the
contractum $\mathit{body}[x\mathbin{:=}a] \in \operatorname{Pos}(M)$. Positions are
taken up to a position congruence $\approx$ (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}), the
default being syntactic identity (the finest instance), and
$\operatorname{Pos}(M)$ is generated simultaneously with $\Sh$
(Definition~\ref{def:shapeop}) as one least fixpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Rational term]\label{def:rationalterm}
$M$ is \emph{rational with respect to $\approx$} if the quotient $\Posq{M}$ is finite.
For the finest congruence this is $\operatorname{Pos}(M)$ finite; a coarser sound congruence can
make rational a term the finest one does not (Theorem~\ref{thm:termination}).
\end{definition}

\subsection{The shape relation}
The development is parametric in a \emph{head-evaluation}, presented as a first-order operator on
the positions of a term. We isolate the property it must have, then fix the weak-head instance
used throughout.

\begin{definition}[(H-fo) head-evaluation]\label{def:hfo}
A \emph{first-order head-evaluation} assigns to each node either a single outermost
$\Sigma$-constructor, with its children named among the node's sub-positions, or the shape of one
sub-position (a head redex). It is \emph{(H-fo)} when these clauses are \emph{positive}
(Remark~\ref{rem:positivity}), so the induced operator $T^{\Sh}$ (Definition~\ref{def:shapeop}) is
monotone and $\Sh := \operatorname{lfp}(T^{\Sh})$ exists; the tree's meaningless set is then
$\{n : \Sh(n)=\bot\}$ \emph{by definition}. The weak-head shape (Definition~\ref{def:sh}) is the
instance we use throughout; head reduction under $\lambda$ is another. Every definition, theorem,
and proof below is stated for an arbitrary (H-fo) head-evaluation.
\end{definition}

A second ingredient is also a parameter, and the rest of the paper turns on it: the
\emph{identity} of positions, the equality on which a re-entry is recognised and a cycle folded.
Syntactic identity (Section~\ref{sec:congruence}) is one choice, the finest. We isolate the
property it must have, exactly as we did for the head-evaluation.

\begin{definition}[Position congruence]\label{def:congruence}
A \emph{position congruence} is an equivalence relation $\approx$ on $\operatorname{Pos}(M)$ that
respects the descent (Definition~\ref{def:path}): if $n \approx n'$ then $\Sh(n)$ and $\Sh(n')$
carry the same outermost label of the full signature, counting $\bot$ as a label that must match
and $\Varsh_i$ with its index, and their demanded sub-positions (the children of $\Sh(n)$, the
readout descent, not the raw syntactic subterms) are pairwise $\approx$-related. Being a
congruence, $\approx$ lifts the shape operator $T^{\Sh}$ (Definition~\ref{def:shapeop}) and the
readout functional $\Psi$ (Definition~\ref{def:readoutfun}) to the quotient
$\operatorname{Pos}(M)/{\approx}$, where they remain monotone, so the least fixpoints are computed
over the quotient. Syntactic (de Bruijn) identity is the finest congruence and the default
instance; tree equality (Definition~\ref{def:btree}) is the coarsest. Between them lie congruence
modulo a decidable theory and bisimulation. Every definition, theorem, and proof below is stated
for an arbitrary position congruence, with syntactic identity as the default.
\end{definition}

The congruence is on the \emph{demanded descent}, so two positions may be $\approx$-identified
while differing in a subterm the readout never descends into. This is what lets a coarser
congruence fold positions that syntactic identity keeps apart (Section~\ref{sec:valrec}).

\begin{remark}[Soundness is the congruence law]\label{rem:soundness}
A congruence is \emph{sound} when it is contained in tree equality: $n \approx n'$ implies
$\Btree(n)=\Btree(n')$, so folding by $\approx$ never changes the denotation. Write tree equality
as the greatest fixpoint $\nu\Phi$ of the behavioural operator $\Phi$ on relations, where
$n\,\Phi(R)\,n'$ holds iff $\Sh(n),\Sh(n')$ share the outermost label and their demanded children
are pairwise $R$-related; then $\Btree(n)=\Btree(n')$ unfolds coinductively to exactly $\nu\Phi$.
The congruence law of Definition~\ref{def:congruence} \emph{is} $\approx\,\subseteq\,\Phi(\approx)$,
so by coinduction $\approx\,\subseteq\,\nu\Phi$: a congruence stated over the full signature is
sound by construction. The only load-bearing clause is that $\bot$ must match a non-$\bot$ only
with itself; folding $\bot$ into the outermost label makes the congruence law and soundness
coincide. Syntactic identity is sound because it is the trivial $\Phi$-invariant: $\Sh$ is a
function, so equal positions have equal shapes and identical children. To establish a coarser
$\approx$ one discharges the same $\Phi$-invariant, either by congruence-closing tree-equal
generators (the inductive instances), by exhibiting a bisimulation (the coinductive instances), or
by a tree-preserving canonical map whose kernel it is (the dead-subterm fold of
Section~\ref{sec:valrec}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Weak-head shape, an (H-fo) instance]\label{def:sh}
The \emph{weak-head} shape relation $\Sh$ assigns to a node its outermost constructor after weak
head reduction:
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Sh(\,\mathit{body}[x \mathbin{:=} a]\,)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,\mathit{body}) && \text{(head redex)}\\
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Appsh(f,a)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) \text{ is rigid} && \text{(root-stable)}\\
  \Sh(\Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)) &= \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N) && &&\\
  \Sh(\Varsh(x)) &= \Varsh(x). && &&
\end{aligned}
\]
A node is \emph{rigid} when its head is a variable or is itself root-stable
(non-abstraction). This instance performs \emph{weak} head reduction only: it stops at
the outermost constructor and does not reduce under $\lambda$. How far the head-evaluation
reduces is itself the parameter (Definition~\ref{def:hfo}), not a fixed property of $\Sh$:
the head-reduction-under-$\lambda$ instance reduces under the leading abstractions to expose
the head, giving the B\"ohm rather than the L\'evy--Longo tree (Section~\ref{sec:related}),
so a node whose body has no head normal form (such as $\lambda x.\,\Omega$) is $\bot$ there
while this instance reads it as $\lambda x.\,\bot$. No (H-fo) instance reduces inside
arguments; the readout descends into them instead.
\end{definition}

\noindent
$\Sh$ is first-order: it is a relation over nodes, with no quantification over a
function space.

\begin{remark}[Positivity]\label{rem:positivity}
The clauses of $\Sh$ are \emph{definite}: every premise is an atom $\Sh(\cdot)=c$,
and there is no negation. In particular ``rigid'' must be read as the positive
disjunction ``head is a $\Varsh$, or head is itself a root-stable $\Appsh$'', never
as the negative ``head is not a $\Lamsh$''; a negation-as-failure reading would
break positivity and forfeit the least-fixpoint semantics below. Positivity makes
the immediate-consequence operator $T_P$ monotone (indeed continuous), so by
Knaster--Tarski its least fixpoint exists and is unique, and the least-fixpoint
reading $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ of Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings} is well-defined and sound by
construction \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}. Soundness of $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ therefore costs
nothing beyond fixing this positive presentation; the work of the paper is
elsewhere (Sections~\ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:application}).
\end{remark}

\noindent
Definition~\ref{def:sh} is made precise as the least fixpoint of an operator on the
flat domain of shapes, so that ``$\Sh$ undefined'' is exactly the value $\bot$ at a
non-terminating weak head reduction.

\begin{definition}[Shape operator]\label{def:shapeop}
Let $C = \{\Varsh_i\}_{i\in\mathbb{N}} \cup \{\Lamsh(N)\}_{N} \cup
\{\Appsh(f,a)\}_{f,a} \cup \{\bot\}$ be the flat domain of \emph{shapes}, ordered by
$\bot \sqsubseteq c$ for all $c$ and no other strict relation. The \emph{shape
operator} $T^{\Sh}\colon (\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to C) \to
(\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to C)$ is
\[
T^{\Sh}(s)(n) =
\begin{cases}
  \Varsh_i & n=\Varsh_i\\
  \Lamsh(N) & n=\Lamsh(\lambda x.N)\\
  s\big(\mathit{body}[x\mathbin{:=}a]\big) & n=\Appsh(f,a),\ s(f)=\Lamsh(\lambda x.\,\mathit{body})\\
  \Appsh(f,a) & n=\Appsh(f,a),\ s(f)\in\{\Varsh_i,\Appsh(\cdot,\cdot)\}\\
  \bot & n=\Appsh(f,a),\ s(f)=\bot.
\end{cases}
\]
Define $\Sh := \operatorname{lfp}(T^{\Sh})$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Shape is well-defined]\label{prop:shwd}
$T^{\Sh}$ is monotone and $\omega$-continuous under the pointwise order, so by Kleene
$\Sh = \bigsqcup_{k\in\mathbb{N}} (T^{\Sh})^{k}(\bot)$ exists and is its least
fixpoint, and $\Sh(n)=\bot$ exactly when the head-evaluation from $n$ does not
terminate (here, weak head reduction). Proof in Appendix~\ref{app:shape}.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Positions are tree addresses}
\begin{definition}[Path]\label{def:path}
The descent alphabet is $\Delta = \{\dlam,\dhead,\darg\}$. A \emph{path} is a word
in $\Delta^{*}$. The valid paths of $M$ and the subterm $\subt{M}{p}$ at a valid
path $p$ are defined coinductively together with $\Sh$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\varepsilon$ is valid and $\subt{M}{\varepsilon} = M$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)$ then $p\,\dlam$ is valid and
        $\subt{M}{p\,\dlam} = N$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(R,Q)$ then $p\,\dhead$ and $p\,\darg$ are
        valid, $\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}=R$ and $\subt{M}{p\,\darg}=Q$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)$ then $p$ is a leaf;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p})$ is undefined then $p$ is a leaf.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\noindent
A path is purely a static address into the tree being built; all dynamics (which
positions exist, where divergence occurs) are delegated to $\Sh$.

\begin{definition}[Tree readout]\label{def:btree}
\[
\Btree(\subt{M}{p}) =
\begin{cases}
  x & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)\\
  \lambda.\;\Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dlam}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\cdots)\\
  \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}) \cdot \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\darg}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(\cdots)\\
  \bot & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) \text{ undefined.}
\end{cases}
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Definitions~\ref{def:sh}--\ref{def:btree} present the tree of the chosen (H-fo) head-evaluation as
the readout of $\Sh$. The design point is load-bearing: a $\bot$-headed application collapses to
$\bot$ (Definition~\ref{def:shapeop}), so the meaningless set is exactly the least-fixpoint
$\bot$. This places the construction in the \emph{collapsing} family: with the weak head reduction
used here it is the L\'evy--Longo tree, and with head reduction under $\lambda$ it is the B\"ohm
tree \citeTODO{barendregt, levy-longo}. The Berarducci tree, which \emph{retains} $\bot$-headed
applications, is \emph{not} (H-fo): retaining them is non-monotone, so it has no least-fixpoint
$\Sh$ (Section~\ref{sec:related}).
\end{remark}

\section{The least-fixpoint reading of the shape relation}\label{sec:tworeadings}
% =====================================================================
$\Sh$ assigns each position its shape: a deterministic calculus exposes one constructor. The
readout reads one constructor off $\Sh$ and defers to its children; its functional $\Psi$
(Definition~\ref{def:readoutfun}) is monotone, and the tree is the least fixpoint
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P) = \operatorname{lfp}(\Psi)$, the limit of the Kleene ascent that fills
$\bot$-holes from $\bot$ upward (Definition~\ref{def:lfpreading}).

The reading is made precise as the least fixpoint of one readout functional.

\begin{definition}[Readout functional]\label{def:readoutfun}
The \emph{readout functional} $\Psi\colon (\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to D) \to
(\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to D)$ reads one constructor off $\Sh$ and defers to its
argument for the children:
\[
\Psi(g)(n) =
\begin{cases}
  \bot & \Sh(n)=\bot\\
  \Varsh_i & \Sh(n)=\Varsh_i\\
  \Lamsh\big(g(N)\big) & \Sh(n)=\Lamsh(\lambda x.N)\\
  \Appsh\big(g(f),g(a)\big) & \Sh(n)=\Appsh(f,a).
\end{cases}
\]
By Proposition~\ref{prop:cpo} the pointwise order on $\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to D$ is a
pointed $\omega$-CPO and $\Psi$ is monotone and $\omega$-continuous.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Least-fixpoint reading]\label{def:lfpreading}
The \emph{least-fixpoint reading} is
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)(M) := \operatorname{lfp}(\Psi)(M) =
\bigsqcup_{k\in\mathbb{N}} \Psi^{k}(\lambda n.\,\bot_D)(M)$. It merges partial
trees from $\bot$ upward: each iterate fills the $\bot$-holes of the
previous one, and because positions are taken up to the congruence a guarded re-entry folds into the
finite rational representation of the subtree it generates.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Meaningless leaf]\label{rem:codomains}
The tree's meaningless leaf is $\bot$: an unproductive cycle is $\bot$, a position whose
head-evaluation does not terminate.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[When a readout terminates]\label{rem:termination}
A single readout terminates iff the term has finitely many distinct positions up to $\approx$,
that is, iff its tree is \emph{rational} with respect to $\approx$. An infinite but
rational tree, the lazy cyclic list $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$
written $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$, folds under $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ to a node that points to itself,
a finite cyclic graph, and terminates. Two failure modes are distinct. A \emph{non-rational}
infinite tree, the stream of all naturals $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;1\;\cdots)$, has
unboundedly many distinct subtrees and hence no finite representation under \emph{any} sound
congruence; the readout produces ever-new positions and does not terminate. A rational tree may
still diverge under the \emph{finest} congruence yet fold under a coarser one
(Theorem~\ref{thm:termination}). Termination is governed by rationality relative to $\approx$.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Termination]\label{thm:termination}
For every term $M$ and decidable sound congruence $\approx$, the term is rational with respect to
$\approx$ ($\Posq{M}$ finite, Definition~\ref{def:rationalterm}) if and only if its readout halts;
and in that case $\lfpT(M)$ is a rational tree (Definition~\ref{def:rational}). The converse of the
last implication fails for the finest congruence, and the gap demonstrates the parameter's
spectrum: syntactic identity is strictly finer than tree equality, so syntactically distinct
positions may share a subtree, and tree rationality does not bound $\operatorname{Pos}(M)$
(Appendix~\ref{app:termination} gives a witness, a rational stream with infinitely many distinct
tail positions, on which the finest congruence diverges while a coarser sound congruence, in the
limit tree equality, folds it to a finite quotient). When $M$ is not rational with respect to $\approx$ the
readout enumerates infinitely many distinct classes and diverges. Proof in
Appendix~\ref{app:termination}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Folding the converse-failure witness]\label{rem:deadfold}
The witness $M=Y\,F\,\underline 0$ with $F=\lambda s.\lambda n.\,\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(s\,(\mathtt{succ}\;n))$
has a constant head and threads the index $n$ through an argument the readout never demands, so its
tail positions $Y\,F\,\underline k$ are pairwise syntactically distinct yet all denote the one
constant-$0$ stream. The coarser congruence that folds it identifies positions differing only in a
\emph{dead} subterm, one occurring only in argument slots of the recursion (Remark~\ref{rem:soundness},
the canonical-map route). It is sound because a dead subterm cannot reach the tree, strictly coarser
than syntactic identity (it folds the witness), and strictly finer than tree equality (it folds only
by deadness, never by deciding arbitrary tree equality). Recognising deadness exactly is the
neededness problem and undecidable in general, but a conservative decidable analysis certifies it
here, so this is a decidable sound congruence on which $M$ is rational. Neither congruence closure nor
bisimulation reaches it: the identity $Y\,F\,\underline j \approx Y\,F\,\underline k$ is coinductive
and the presentation is infinite, so the fold needs the dead subterm erased, a tree-preserving map,
not a closure. The implementation\anon[~(supplementary material)]{~\cite{firstorderlambda}}
realises this fold as its dead-subterm congruence,
recognising the dead slot by a sound recursion-argument rule, the only one of its four congruences
that finitises the witness; the bisimulation instance, folding exactly the rational fragment,
still diverges on it.
\end{remark}

\section{Adequacy: \texorpdfstring{$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$}{lfp(T\_P)} is the tree}
\label{sec:grounding}
% =====================================================================
\begin{theorem}[Adequacy]\label{thm:adequacy}
For every term $M$ and sound position congruence $\approx$ (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}), the
least-fixpoint readout $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)(M)$ over $\Posq{M}$ (Definition~\ref{def:lfpreading}) is,
in $D$, the tree of the (H-fo) head-evaluation (Definition~\ref{def:hfo}) at $M$: it agrees node
for node with the coinductive readout that the head-evaluation enumerates. Soundness is what makes
this hold over the quotient, since every position in a class then shares one tree. Proof in
Appendix~\ref{app:adequacy}.
\end{theorem}

\noindent
Because we work on raw terms, there is no normal-form translation to justify and
no administrative-redex bridge: $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is the tree essentially by construction,
and the only content is that Definitions~\ref{def:path}--\ref{def:btree} present
the head-evaluation faithfully.

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$}{lfp(T\_P)}: the least fixpoint by merging partial trees}
\label{sec:valrec}
% =====================================================================
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is the least fixpoint of $\Sh$, computed by merging partial
trees from $\bot$ upward (Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings}). Because positions are
taken up to the congruence, a position re-entered during the computation of its own shape is
detected by its identity test, and the behaviour splits on whether that re-entry is productive.

\begin{definition}[Cyclic, guarded, and unproductive positions]\label{def:guarded}
A position $n \in \operatorname{Pos}(M)$ is \emph{cyclic} if a position $\approx$-equal to it
occurs strictly below it on some readout path, i.e.\ $n \approx \subt{n}{p}$ for some non-empty
valid path $p$ (Definition~\ref{def:path}); for the finest congruence this is syntactic recurrence
$n = \subt{n}{p}$, and a coarser one folds a re-entry into the same class even when the two
positions differ (the dead-subterm witness, Remark~\ref{rem:deadfold}). The condition is
$\approx$-invariant, since $\approx$ preserves the outermost label of $\Sh$. A cyclic $n$ is
\emph{guarded} if $\Sh(n) \in \{\Lamsh(\cdot),\Appsh(\cdot,\cdot)\}$, so a constructor is exposed at
$n$ before the re-entry, and \emph{unproductive} if $\Sh(n)=\bot$, so the head-evaluation of $n$
returns to its class with no constructor exposed.
\end{definition}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Guarded.} $\Sh(n)$ is a constructor and the re-entry sits in a
        child position. Under $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ the merge folds at the re-entry, yielding a
        \emph{rational} (regular) infinite tree, finitely represented as a node that
        points to itself, where head reduction would unfold forever.
  \item \textbf{Unproductive.} $\Sh(n)$ returns to $n$ with no constructor exposed;
        its least fixpoint is $\bot$: the limit does not improve an unproductive cycle.
\end{itemize}

\begin{example}[Guarded: the cyclic stream $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$]\label{ex:cons}
Write the cyclic stream of zeros $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$ as $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$,
with the Scott encoding $\mathtt{cons} = \lambda h.\lambda t.\lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,h\,t$.
Then $\Sh(r) = \lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,0\,r'$, derived in finitely many steps
without re-requiring the head $\Sh(r)$: the recursive occurrence enters only as
data. The structurally repeating cell is one position in the quotient. Under $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ the
merge folds at the guarded re-entry,
\[
  \rho \;=\; \lambda c.\,\lambda n.\,(c\cdot 0)\cdot \rho,
\]
the finite representation of the rational tree
$\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\cdots))$, a node
pointing to itself, where head reduction would unfold forever. No recursion
binder is needed; $Y$ produces the repetition that the congruence folds.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Unproductive: $\Omega$ and $Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$]\label{ex:xx}
$\Omega = (\lambda x.\,x\,x)(\lambda x.\,x\,x)$ head-reduces to itself with no
constructor exposed, returning to the same position; so $\Omega = \bot$, the
meaningless leaf of its tree. The least fixpoint settles at $\bot$, since there is no
constructor for the merge to add, and the readout terminates in finite time where head reduction
loops. The same holds for $Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$, the pure-$\lambda$ rendering of
$\mathtt{letrec}\;x = x$.
\end{example}

\paragraph{Why the bottom is $\bot$, and why that is sound}
The merge ascends from a bottom value, and that bottom must be a fresh $\bot$,
distinct from every $\lambda$-term, not a reused value. Otherwise the resolution
test would be ambiguous: a position still sitting at the bottom would be
indistinguishable from one that genuinely computed that value, and a round could read
a dependency's placeholder as a real value and reach a \emph{wrong} fixed point. With
$\bot$ a fresh leaf the test is exact: a position is resolved precisely when its value
is no longer $\bot$. The only test of identity, the one that lets the merge
fold a cycle, is the decidable position congruence, not the undecidable
$\beta$-equality of values.

\section{Applications}

\subsection{Decidability and dissolved problems}\label{sec:variants}
On the rational fragment the tree is a finite graph, so its equality is decidable; and a cluster of
problems that need added machinery in other settings does not arise here.

\begin{corollary}[Decidable tree equality on the rational fragment]\label{cor:decidable}
Restricted to terms rational with respect to a decidable sound congruence
(Definition~\ref{def:rationalterm}), equality of the trees
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)(M) = \mathrm{lfp}(T_P)(N)$ is decidable. By Theorem~\ref{thm:termination} each
tree is a finite rational graph over the position quotient $\Posq{M}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy});
these graphs are computable, since the head-evaluation terminates at each position
(Proposition~\ref{prop:shwd}) and the position set closes after finitely many steps, while
position identity is the chosen decidable congruence (syntactic equality of de Bruijn terms for the
finest instance). Equality of two rational trees given by finite graphs is then decided by the
standard cycle-detecting bisimulation \citeTODO{courcelle}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Hard problems dissolved]\label{cor:dissolved}
Several problems that need added machinery in other settings do not arise here.
Observing sharing or cyclicity in a pure language needs an observable-sharing
primitive or a circular-program idiom \citeTODO{gill, bird}; here sharing is the
chosen position congruence. Representing a cyclic structure needs an explicit
recursion binder or a cyclic $\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{ariola-klop}; here $Y$
generates the repetition and the congruence folds it, with no binder added to the
calculus. Deciding equality over cyclic structures needs a separate unification
discipline or recursive-type system \citeTODO{colmerauer, amadio-cardelli,
brandt-henglein}; here it is cycle detection over positions within the calculus
(Corollary~\ref{cor:decidable}). Terminating a recursive query over a cyclic datum
needs a tabled evaluator \citeTODO{tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}; here the query's own
applications fall into the same classes and fold (Section~\ref{sec:reach}).
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Structural computation over cyclic data}
\label{sec:reach}
Before any query, constructing the cyclic datum is already the new step. The pure term
$Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$ denotes the infinite stream $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$,
and under head reduction it has no finite form, unfolding forever. Up to the congruence it has
finitely many positions, so $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ gives it a finite \emph{circular} representation, a
singly-linked cell whose tail points back to itself (Example~\ref{ex:cons}). A circular linked list
from a pure $\lambda$-term is what the standard semantics cannot build: elsewhere a cycle needs
mutable references, an explicit recursion binder, or an observable-sharing primitive
(Section~\ref{sec:related}); here $Y$ produces the repetition and the congruence folds it, with
nothing added to the calculus.

A recursive query over that datum is then an ordinary $\lambda$-function, and the guarded versus
unproductive split of Definition~\ref{def:guarded} governs the \emph{query} just as it governs the
data. Take first the naive walk
\[
  \mathtt{reach} \;=\; Y\,\big(\lambda\mathit{self}.\,\lambda\mathit{lst}.\;
    \mathit{lst}\;(\lambda h.\lambda t.\,\mathit{self}\;t)\;\present\big),
\]
which recurses down a Scott stream looking for $\mathtt{nil}$. Applied to the cyclic
stream $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, each recursive call builds the application
$\mathtt{reach}\;t$; because $r$ has finitely many positions up to the congruence, these
applications fall into finitely many classes, so the least fixpoint folds the
recursion. Head reduction, which identifies nothing, builds a fresh application at every
step and diverges; $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ folds at the repeated position and returns a
definite value (here $\bot$: no $\mathtt{nil}$ is ever reached, so the walk is itself
an unproductive cycle). The query terminates for the same reason the readout does:
finitely many positions up to the congruence.
It is also memoised for free, since the congruence shares the repeated subcomputations.
The walk is unguarded: its recursive call $\mathit{self}\;t$ exposes no constructor before
re-entering, so it is the unproductive arm of Definition~\ref{def:guarded}, and $\bot$ is the
correct answer, decided in finite time.

\paragraph{Mapping a cyclic list}
The guarded arm of the same query produces rather than decides. The textbook
$\mathtt{map}\,f = Y\,(\lambda\mathit{self}.\,\lambda\mathit{lst}.\;\mathit{lst}\;
(\lambda h.\lambda t.\,\mathtt{cons}\;(f\,h)\;(\mathit{self}\;t))\;\mathtt{nil})$ has nothing
cycle-aware in it. Its recursive call sits under a $\mathtt{cons}$, so it is guarded: applied to the
cyclic stream $r=\mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, the recursive application $\mathtt{map}\,f\;t$ re-enters the
same closed position $\mathtt{map}\,f\;r$, and $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ folds it. The result is a finite
circular list $\rho=\mathtt{cons}\;(f\,0)\;\rho$, every element transformed, where head reduction
would map the infinite stream forever. The fold is the lazy knot-tying: each cell is registered
before its tail is recursed into, so the congruence supplies the table. On a finite list the same
$\mathtt{map}$ is the ordinary one. The implementation\anon[~(supplementary
material)]{~\cite{firstorderlambda}} reproduces both arms: the naive walk terminates with value
$\bot$ on $r$, and $\mathtt{map}\;\mathtt{succ}$ folds $r$ into the finite circular stream of $1$s,
where head reduction loops.

\subsection{Memoising arbitrary programs}
\label{sec:memo}
More generally, $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is the denotation of a \emph{memoised} run of any
$\lambda$-program. Memoisation needs a comparable key on which to recognise a
repeated call; the higher-order view offers none, since value equality of functions
is undecidable. The first-order reading replaces values by positions whose
identity is a decidable congruence, so it is the natural setting in which memoisation is
even definable. The congruence is that memoisation: it is what lets a re-entry be
recognised at all. Head reduction, which remembers nothing, unfolds a cyclic position
forever; the least fixpoint $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ recognises the re-entry and folds, taking the
limit. The two are the unfolding and its limit, separated by how much they remember.

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}
% =====================================================================
\paragraph{Trees and infinitary $\lambda$-calculus.}
The Berarducci, L\'evy--Longo, and B\"ohm trees form a hierarchy by their
meaningless sets \citeTODO{berarducci, levy-longo, barendregt}; the infinitary
$\lambda$-calculus framework \citeTODO{kennaway-klop-sleep-devries} situates them
uniformly. Our construction gives the tree of any (H-fo) head-evaluation: with weak head
reduction it is the L\'evy--Longo tree, with head reduction under $\lambda$ the B\"ohm tree. The
Berarducci tree falls outside (H-fo): it retains $\bot$-headed applications, which is non-monotone,
so it has no least-fixpoint $\Sh$.

\paragraph{Least Herbrand models and tabling.}
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is the least Herbrand model of a definite program
\citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}; SLD resolution diverges on a cyclic query, and a tabled evaluator
\citeTODO{tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}
computes $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ by completing unproductive cycles instead of looping. Tabling is
the canonical mechanism for terminating left-recursive and cyclic fixpoint queries
that plain resolution diverges on; our query application
(Section~\ref{sec:reach}) is an instance of it transported into the
$\lambda$-calculus.

\paragraph{Rational trees and recursive types.}
The capability of deciding structural queries over cyclic structures by
cycle detection is long established outside the $\lambda$-calculus. Rational
(regular) infinite trees and their algebra \citeTODO{courcelle} underlie cyclic
unification in logic programming \citeTODO{colmerauer}, where the occurs-check is
dropped and cyclic terms are first class. In type theory, equirecursive
(\emph{regular}) recursive types give terminating algorithms for subtyping and
equality precisely by detecting cycles in the type's regular tree
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. These are the same phenomenon as our
guarded-cycle case---a rational denotation with finitely many distinct
subtrees---reached by different routes. The point of difference is one of
position rather than capability: those approaches add machinery (a unification
discipline, a separate type-level system), whereas $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is a re-reading of the
$\lambda$-calculus's own tree semantics, obtained by choosing a sound position congruence
(syntactic identity is the finest) and taking a least fixpoint, with nothing added to the calculus
(Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy}).

\paragraph{The spectrum of position congruences}
The congruence parameter (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}) is bounded below by syntactic identity
and above by tree equality, and the two ends have opposite fixpoint character. The inductive end is
congruence closure of tree-equal generators, realised by e-graphs and equality saturation
\citeTODO{nelson-oppen, egraphs}; it folds whatever finitely many sound equations generate, the
tabled and least-Herbrand reading of Section~\ref{sec:reach} \citeTODO{tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}.
The coinductive end is bisimulation, tree equality being its greatest fixpoint; deciding it on the
rational fragment is exactly the cyclic-tree and equirecursive-type procedure
\citeTODO{courcelle, amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}, and computing it during construction is
coinductive resolution \citeTODO{simon-coinductive}. A congruence closure cannot reach a
non-finitely-generated identity, so the converse-failure witness (Remark~\ref{rem:deadfold}) folds
only by a third route, a tree-preserving canonical map that erases the dead subterm, certified by a
neededness analysis. Syntactic identity is the finest, cheapest point on this line; the others trade
a richer identity test for a larger rational fragment.

\paragraph{Cyclic $\lambda$-terms and recursion.}
Cyclic graphs / explicit recursion \citeTODO{ariola-klop} and the call-by-need
$\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{ariola-felleisen} provide the sharing on which
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$'s folding rests; the guarded-cycle case yields rational trees,
and the productivity distinction is the standard one from infinitary rewriting.

\paragraph{Sharing in functional languages.}
That a pure functional language cannot observe sharing or cyclicity is a known
obstacle: a traversal of a cyclic structure diverges because referential
transparency hides the repetition. Existing remedies \emph{add} something to the
language, such as observable sharing \citeTODO{gill} or the deliberate use of
laziness in circular programs \citeTODO{bird}. We add nothing to the calculus:
quotienting the positions exposes the repetition, and $\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ folds it.

\paragraph{Higher-order model checking.}
Deciding properties of the (possibly infinite) trees generated by higher-order
programs \citeTODO{ong-hors, kobayashi} shares the goal of answering queries about
$\lambda$-generated infinite trees, but proceeds by automata and games over
B\"ohm-tree-like denotations rather than by a least-model reading of a shape
relation.

\paragraph{Lazy $\lambda$-calculus.}
The lazy $\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{abramsky-ong} observes weak head normal
forms; its tree is the L\'evy--Longo tree, which is the weak-head (H-fo) instance used here.

\paragraph{Defunctionalization.}
Unlike defunctionalization \citeTODO{reynolds}, which compiles a higher-order
program into a first-order interpreter, we give a first-order semantic
\emph{object} for the higher-order calculus; no interpreter is constructed.

\paragraph{Position.}
The capability we exploit---terminating structural computation over cyclic
data by cycle detection---is decades old in logic programming
\citeTODO{colmerauer, tamaki-sato} and in recursive-type theory
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. What appears to be new is its
position: it is obtained by quotienting the positions of the $\lambda$-calculus's own
tree semantics by a sound congruence and taking a least fixpoint, without leaving the calculus
or adding primitives. That least fixpoint is the same tree, computed effectively
(Section~\ref{sec:grounding}): it terminates on every structure rational with respect to the chosen
congruence, coarser congruences covering more, and decides an unproductive cycle as $\bot$ in finite
time, where head reduction diverges.

% =====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =====================================================================
The tree of a monotone first-order head-evaluation is the readout of a first-order shape relation,
the least fixpoint of its monotone consequence operator. Head reduction unfolds that fixpoint and
diverges on a cyclic term; the least fixpoint, computed by merging partial trees over
positions up to the congruence, folds it, so a lazy infinite list becomes a node that points to
itself and the readout terminates on every tree rational with respect to that congruence. So
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ is an adequate monotone refinement of head reduction: the same tree, computed
effectively.

\section{The tree domain (Proposition~\ref{prop:cpo})}\label{app:domain}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:cpo}]
\emph{Least element.} For any $t \in D$, $\operatorname{dom}(\bot_D)=\{\varepsilon\}
\subseteq \operatorname{dom}(t)$ and $\bot_D(\varepsilon)=\bot$, so
$\bot_D \sqsubseteq t$ by Definition~\ref{def:order}.

\emph{Directed completeness.} Let $\{t_i\}_{i\in I}$ be $\sqsubseteq$-directed. Define
$t$ by $\operatorname{dom}(t)=\bigcup_i \operatorname{dom}(t_i)$ and, for
$p \in \operatorname{dom}(t)$, $t(p)=t_i(p)$ if $t_i(p)\ne\bot$ for some $i$, else
$t(p)=\bot$. This is well-defined: if $t_i(p)\ne\bot\ne t_j(p)$, directedness gives
$t_\ell \sqsupseteq t_i,t_j$, and by Definition~\ref{def:order} a non-$\bot$ label is
preserved upward, so $t_i(p)=t_\ell(p)=t_j(p)$. And $t \in D$: $\operatorname{dom}(t)$
is prefix-closed as a union of prefix-closed sets, and arity-respecting because if
$t(p)=\Lamsh$ then $t_i(p)=\Lamsh$ for some $i$, whose children of $p$ are exactly
$\{p\,\dlam\}$, and no other $t_j$ adds a child (labels agree where non-$\bot$, and
$\bot$ has no children); the $\Appsh$ and leaf cases are identical. Finally $t$ is the
least upper bound: each $t_i \sqsubseteq t$, and any upper bound $u$ satisfies
$t \sqsubseteq u$ since at $p$ with $t(p)\ne\bot$ the witnessing $t_i$ gives
$t(p)=t_i(p)=u(p)$. In particular every $\omega$-chain has a least upper bound.
\end{proof}

\section{The shape operator (Proposition~\ref{prop:shwd})}\label{app:shape}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:shwd}]
Write $T=T^{\Sh}$, with $\operatorname{Pos}(M)\to C$ ordered pointwise over the flat
order on $C$.

\emph{Monotone.} Let $s\sqsubseteq s'$ and fix $n$. For $n=\Varsh_i$ or
$n=\Lamsh(\lambda x.N)$, $T(s)(n)=T(s')(n)$. For $n=\Appsh(f,a)$ the clause is selected
by $s(f)$: if $s(f)=\bot$ then $T(s)(n)=\bot\sqsubseteq T(s')(n)$; if $s(f)\ne\bot$
then flatness forces $s'(f)=s(f)$, so the same clause fires, giving
$T(s)(n)=\Appsh(f,a)=T(s')(n)$ in the rigid subcase and
$T(s)(n)=s(\mathit{body}[x{:=}a])\sqsubseteq s'(\mathit{body}[x{:=}a])=T(s')(n)$ in the
head-redex subcase.

\emph{Continuous.} Let $s_0\sqsubseteq s_1\sqsubseteq\cdots$ with $s=\bigsqcup_k s_k$.
By monotonicity $\bigsqcup_k T(s_k)\sqsubseteq T(s)$. Conversely, fix $n$ with
$T(s)(n)\ne\bot$. Computing $T(s)(n)$ inspects at most two arguments of $s$, namely
$s(f)$ and, in the head-redex subcase, $s(\mathit{body}[x{:=}a])$; each is non-$\bot$
here, hence by flatness equals $s_k$ at that argument for all large $k$. Taking $k$
past both, $T(s_k)(n)=T(s)(n)$, so $T(s)(n)\sqsubseteq\bigsqcup_k T(s_k)(n)$. Thus $T$
is $\omega$-continuous and, by the Kleene fixed-point theorem,
$\Sh=\operatorname{lfp}(T)=\bigsqcup_k T^k(\bot)$.

\emph{Characterisation.} The iterates resolve shapes bottom-up: $T^k(\bot)(n)\ne\bot$
iff $n$ has a shape derivation under Definition~\ref{def:shapeop} of height below $k$,
where each head-redex clause first resolves the head $f$ and then its contractum. Such
a finite derivation exists iff the head-evaluation from $n$ reaches a normal
form ($\Varsh$, $\Lamsh$, or an $\Appsh$ with rigid head). \TODO{spell out the height
measure: an induction on $k$ matching ``$T^k(\bot)(n)\ne\bot$'' with ``the head shape
of $n$, and in the redex case the contractum's shape, are resolved within $k$
iterations'', concluding $\Sh(n)=\bot$ iff the head-evaluation from $n$ diverges. The
statement and both endpoints are fixed; only the bookkeeping of the measure remains.}
\end{proof}

\section{Adequacy (Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy})}\label{app:adequacy}
\begin{lemma}[Meaningless leaves]\label{lem:rootactive}
$\Sh(n)=\bot$ iff the tree at $n$ is $\bot$.
\end{lemma}
\noindent
By Proposition~\ref{prop:shwd}, $\Sh(n)=\bot$ iff the head-evaluation from $n$ does not
terminate. For an (H-fo) head-evaluation (Definition~\ref{def:hfo}) this is the meaningless set
\emph{by definition}: the tree's $\bot$ leaf is exactly a position whose head-evaluation
diverges, so the identification is immediate and needs no external input.

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy}]
Fix a sound congruence $\approx$ (Definition~\ref{def:congruence}). By soundness every position in
a class shares one tree (Remark~\ref{rem:soundness}), so the readout over $\Posq{M}$ and the
per-position readout of Definition~\ref{def:btree} agree, and the induction below is verbatim.
Let $R=\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)=\operatorname{lfp}(\Psi)$. Both $R$ and the tree the head-evaluation
enumerates label a valid path $p$ by a function of $\Sh(\subt{M}{p})$: $\Varsh_i$, $\Lamsh$ (with
child $p\,\dlam$), $\Appsh$ (with children $p\,\dhead,p\,\darg$), or $\bot$
(Definitions~\ref{def:sh}, \ref{def:btree}). By Proposition~\ref{prop:cpo}, $R$ is the unique
tree with $R(\subt{M}{p})= \Psi(R)(\subt{M}{p})$, so $R(M)(p)$ is determined by
$\Sh(\subt{M}{p})$ exactly as the head-evaluation's readout prescribes. By induction on $|p|$,
the domains and labels coincide: the variable, $\Lamsh$, and $\Appsh$ cases match clause by
clause, and the $\bot$-leaf case matches by Lemma~\ref{lem:rootactive}. Hence $R(M)$ is the tree.
\end{proof}

\section{Termination (Theorem~\ref{thm:termination})}\label{app:termination}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:termination}]
The readout descends only through positions of $\operatorname{Pos}(M)$
(Definition~\ref{def:positions}): the constructor children $N,f,a$ and, via the
head-redex clause of $\Sh$, the contracta are exactly its generators.

Throughout, $\approx$ is a decidable sound congruence, so the tabling key (the $\approx$-class) is
testable; the readout descends through classes of $\Posq{M}$.

\emph{Rational $\Rightarrow$ halts.} If $\Posq{M}$ is finite, the
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ computation tables over a finite set and halts.

\emph{Halts $\Rightarrow$ rational.} Contrapositive: if $\Posq{M}$ is
infinite then the descent reaches infinitely many distinct classes and the
$\mathrm{lfp}(T_P)$ tabling never saturates, so it does not halt.

\emph{Rational $\Rightarrow$ tree rational.} The subtrees of $R(M)$ are
$\{R(\subt{M}{p})\}_p=\{R(n):n\in\Posq{M}\}$, finite, so $R(M)$ is
rational (Definition~\ref{def:rational}).

\emph{Failure of the converse for the finest congruence.} The finest congruence identifies
positions by \emph{syntactic} (de Bruijn) identity, which is strictly finer than tree equality, so
the map $n\mapsto R(n)$ need not be injective: syntactically distinct positions can carry the
same tree. A witness is $M=Y\,F\,\underline 0$ with
$F=\lambda s.\lambda n.\,\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(s\,(\mathtt{succ}\;n))$. The head of every
cell is the constant $0$, so $R(M)$ is the rational stream
$\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$ with finitely many distinct subtrees;
but the tail positions $Y\,F\,\underline k$ (with $\underline k=\mathtt{succ}^{k}\,
\underline 0$) are pairwise syntactically distinct, hence distinct classes of the finest
congruence, since $\Sh$ does not reduce the $\mathtt{succ}^{k}\,\underline 0$ buried in the
argument. Hence $\operatorname{Pos}(M)$ is infinite while $\{R(n)\}$ is finite, and the finest
congruence diverges on a rational tree. A coarser sound congruence identifying the tail positions (they differ
only in the dead argument $\mathtt{succ}^{k}\,\underline 0$, so they are tree-equal) makes $\Posq{M}$
finite and the readout halt (Remark~\ref{rem:deadfold}); in the limit tree equality always does. So
tree rationality entails finite $\Posq{M}$ only as $\approx$ approaches tree equality: folding by the
finest position identity is strictly finer than folding by subtree.
\end{proof}
