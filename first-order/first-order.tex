\begin{abstract}
A $\lambda$-term's Berarducci tree is the readout of a single first-order
relation: the weak-head \emph{shape} relation $\Sh$ over the positions of the
term. The shape at a position is single-valued, so $\Sh$ has two resolutions. Read
by head reduction it \emph{is} the Berarducci tree, unfolded; read as a
\emph{least fixpoint} it is computed by merging partial Berarducci trees, partial
trees carrying $\bot$ holes that the merge fills, ascending to the complete tree.
Because positions are interned, so that structural equality is pointer equality, a
guarded cyclic datum has finitely many positions: the least-fixpoint reading folds
it into a finite rational tree and terminates, where head reduction unfolds forever.
The least-fixpoint reading is therefore strictly more defined than the Berarducci
tree, not a conservative extension of it: at an unproductive head cycle the
Berarducci reading diverges ($\bot$) while the least fixpoint stabilizes at a
definite empty value ($\emptyset$), so terms such as $\Omega$, which the Berarducci
tree leaves meaningless, acquire a value. The two readings sit in a convergence
hierarchy whose single knob is the value an unproductive cycle takes, $\bot$ or
$\emptyset$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
The $\lambda$-calculus is the higher-order calculus, yet its tree semantics
factors through a \emph{first-order} relation. We make this precise: the
Berarducci tree $\BeT(M)$ of a term $M$ is the coinductive readout of a single
relation $\Sh$, the \emph{weak-head shape} of a position, which is first-order
in that it ranges over positions (graph nodes) and never over a function space.

Once the tree is presented this way, two observations become immediate. First,
$\Sh$'s clauses are positive, so the immediate-consequence operator that reads off
the tree is monotone. Second, a monotone operator has two readings: the tree it
unfolds by head reduction, and the \emph{least fixpoint} it reaches by merging
partial trees from $\bot$ upward. These come apart only on positions whose sole
support is an unproductive cycle. The head-reduction reading is the Berarducci
tree. The least-fixpoint reading folds a cyclic datum into a finite rational tree
where head reduction unfolds forever, and gives a defined (empty) value where head
reduction diverges; it is therefore strictly more defined.

The two readings are not separate constructions but one knob. The shape at a
position is single-valued (a deterministic calculus exposes one weak-head
constructor), so there is no set to aggregate: the denotation at a position is a
\emph{partial} Berarducci tree, a tree with $\bot$ holes, and the least fixpoint
\emph{merges} these partial trees, filling holes, until the tree stops growing. An
unproductive head cycle has nothing to fill its hole, so it settles at an empty
value. The only free decision is how that empty value reads out. Sending it to
$\bot$ recovers the Berarducci reading; sending it to the term $\lambda a.\,\lambda
b.\,b$, the second Scott constructor, gives the more-defined least-fixpoint reading.
The whole paper is this single choice $\emptyset \mapsto \{\bot,\ \lambda
a.\,\lambda b.\,b\}$: the two readings agree on every productive position and part
only at unproductive cycles, and the first is the one for which Berarducci-tree
equivalence is fully abstract.

The reason any of this is effective is the word in the title. The merge must
recognise when it has returned to a position it is already computing, both to fold
a cycle and to memoise, and recognising a repeated position is a test of
\emph{identity}. On a first-order domain identity is equality of positions, a
comparison of finite paths decidable by a pointer test, and \emph{interning} the
positions makes that pointer test exact; on the higher-order values of the
$\lambda$-calculus identity is $\beta$-equality, which is undecidable. Reading the
tree off a first-order relation is precisely what makes memoisation and the folding
of cyclic structures computable; the higher-order view affords neither.
``First-order'' is not a description of $\Sh$ but the mechanism that the results
rest on.

We never normalise to an intermediate form; in particular we do not use A-normal
form. Positions are read directly off the term, and the dynamics live entirely in
$\Sh$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A presentation of the Berarducci tree as the readout of a first-order
        shape relation $\Sh$ (Sections~\ref{sec:firstorder}, \ref{sec:grounding}).
  \item Two resolutions of $\Sh$: the operational reading $\Sbot$, which we show
        equals the Berarducci tree, and the least-fixpoint reading $\Semp$, computed
        by merging partial Berarducci trees (Sections~\ref{sec:tworeadings},
        \ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:valrec}).
  \item A convergence hierarchy: $\Sbot$ and $\Semp$ agree on every productive
        position and differ only at unproductive head cycles, where $\Sbot$ diverges
        ($\bot$) and $\Semp$ stabilizes at $\emptyset$ (Section~\ref{sec:bridge}).
        $\Semp$ is therefore strictly more defined than $\BeT$, not a conservative
        extension: a self-applicative term such as $\Omega$, meaningless under the
        Berarducci tree, acquires the value $\emptyset$. Once $\Sh$ is given its
        positive presentation (Remark~\ref{rem:positivity}), well-definedness of the
        least fixpoint and the locus of difference are standard fixpoint facts.
  \item As an application, structural computation over cyclic data
        (Section~\ref{sec:application}). Interning the positions folds a guarded
        cyclic datum into a finite rational tree, so an \emph{ordinary} recursive
        query over it terminates where head reduction diverges: the query's own
        recursive applications intern and fold.
\end{itemize}

\section{Overview}\label{sec:overview}
% =====================================================================
Consider a cyclic stream of zeros, written $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, and the pure
$\lambda$-term $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$, which has the \emph{same} Berarducci tree
(the infinite stream $\mathtt{cons}\;0\;(\mathtt{cons}\;0\;\cdots)$). Head reduction
enumerates this tree the same way in both cases, and never finishes.

The least-fixpoint reading folds both. Once positions are interned, structurally
equal positions are one position, so each term has only finitely many distinct
positions: $r$ folds at its single shared cell, and $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$ folds at
the repeating cell its unfolding produces. Both return a finite rational tree, so a
structural computation over either terminates where head reduction diverges. The
folded representations differ but the tree is the same.

The two readings part only at an \emph{unproductive} cycle, a position that returns
to itself before exposing any constructor. Head reduction diverges there ($\bot$);
the least fixpoint stabilizes at $\emptyset$. Such a position is reached not only by
a recursive binder ($\mathtt{letrec}\;x = x$) but by self-application:
$\Omega = (\lambda x.x\,x)(\lambda x.x\,x)$ returns to the same interned position
with no constructor exposed, so the least fixpoint gives $\Omega = \emptyset$ where
its Berarducci tree is $\bot$. The least-fixpoint reading is thus strictly more
defined, and the whole difference is the one knob: the value an unproductive cycle
takes, $\bot$ or $\emptyset$.

\section{Berarducci trees as the readout of a first-order shape relation}
\label{sec:firstorder}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{Terms and interned positions}\label{sec:interning}
We work with pure $\lambda$-terms in de Bruijn form (avoiding $\alpha$-renaming);
a node is labelled $\Lamsh$, $\Appsh$, or $\Varsh$. A \emph{position} is a subterm
reached while computing a shape. Positions are \emph{interned}: two structurally
equal positions are the same object, so identity is a pointer test, not a comparison
of structure. Interning is the mechanism the results rest on. It makes the
recurrence for $\Sh$ a memoisable dynamic program keyed by position, and it makes a
position a finite key on which $\Semp$ folds a cycle: structural equality reduces to
pointer equality. A term has finitely many positions exactly when its Berarducci
tree is \emph{rational} (finitely many distinct subtrees). A recursive definition
produces such repetition directly, and so does a self-application, whose contractum
interns back to an existing position. No recursion binder belongs to the calculus:
the $Y$ combinator produces the repetition that interning folds
(Section~\ref{sec:valrec}).

\subsection{The shape relation}
\begin{definition}[Weak-head shape]\label{def:sh}
The relation $\Sh$ assigns to a node its outermost constructor after weak head
reduction:
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Sh(\,\mathit{body}[x \mathbin{:=} a]\,)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,\mathit{body}) && \text{(head redex)}\\
  \Sh(\Appsh(f,a)) &= \Appsh(f,a)
      && \text{if } \Sh(f) \text{ is rigid} && \text{(root-stable)}\\
  \Sh(\Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)) &= \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N) && &&\\
  \Sh(\Varsh(x)) &= \Varsh(x). && &&
\end{aligned}
\]
A node is \emph{rigid} when its head is a variable or is itself root-stable
(non-abstraction). $\Sh$ performs \emph{weak} head reduction only: it stops at the
outermost constructor and does not reduce under $\lambda$ or inside arguments.
\end{definition}

\noindent
$\Sh$ is first-order: it is a relation over nodes, with no quantification over a
function space.

\begin{remark}[Positivity]\label{rem:positivity}
The clauses of $\Sh$ are \emph{definite}: every premise is an atom $\Sh(\cdot)=c$,
and there is no negation. In particular ``rigid'' must be read as the positive
disjunction ``head is a $\Varsh$, or head is itself a root-stable $\Appsh$'', never
as the negative ``head is not a $\Lamsh$''; a negation-as-failure reading would
break positivity and forfeit the least-fixpoint semantics below. Positivity makes
the immediate-consequence operator $T_{\Sh}$ monotone (indeed continuous), so by
Knaster--Tarski its least fixpoint exists and is unique, and the least-fixpoint
reading $\Semp$ of Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings} is well-defined and sound by
construction \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}. Soundness of $\Semp$ therefore costs
nothing beyond fixing this positive presentation; the work of the paper is
elsewhere (Sections~\ref{sec:grounding}, \ref{sec:application}).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Positions are Berarducci-tree addresses}
\begin{definition}[Path]\label{def:path}
The descent alphabet is $\Delta = \{\dlam,\dhead,\darg\}$. A \emph{path} is a word
in $\Delta^{*}$. The valid paths of $M$ and the subterm $\subt{M}{p}$ at a valid
path $p$ are defined coinductively together with $\Sh$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\varepsilon$ is valid and $\subt{M}{\varepsilon} = M$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\lambda x.\,N)$ then $p\,\dlam$ is valid and
        $\subt{M}{p\,\dlam} = N$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(R,Q)$ then $p\,\dhead$ and $p\,\darg$ are
        valid, $\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}=R$ and $\subt{M}{p\,\darg}=Q$;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)$ then $p$ is a leaf;
  \item if $\Sh(\subt{M}{p})$ is undefined then $p$ is a leaf.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\noindent
A path is purely a static address into the tree being built; all dynamics (which
positions exist, where divergence occurs) are delegated to $\Sh$.

\begin{definition}[Tree readout]\label{def:btree}
\[
\Btree(\subt{M}{p}) =
\begin{cases}
  x & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Varsh(x)\\
  \lambda.\;\Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dlam}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Lamsh(\cdots)\\
  \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\dhead}) \cdot \Btree(\subt{M}{p\,\darg}) & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) = \Appsh(\cdots)\\
  \bot & \Sh(\subt{M}{p}) \text{ undefined.}
\end{cases}
\]
The application case is retained even when the head subtree is $\bot$
(a $\bot$-headed application node is kept, not collapsed).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Definitions~\ref{def:sh}--\ref{def:btree} are the Berarducci tree
\citeTODO{berarducci}, re-presented as the readout of $\Sh$. Two design points are
load-bearing: $\Sh$ observes a \emph{weak} head normal form (it stops at the
outermost $\lambda$), not a head normal form; and the readout \emph{retains}
$\bot$-headed applications. The first places the construction in the
Berarducci/L\'evy--Longo family rather than the B\"ohm family
\citeTODO{barendregt, levy-longo}; the second pins it to Berarducci specifically.
\end{remark}

\section{Two resolutions of the shape relation}\label{sec:tworeadings}
% =====================================================================
$\Sh$ in Definition~\ref{def:sh} assigns each position a \emph{single} shape: a
deterministic calculus exposes one weak-head constructor, so there is no set of
shapes to collect and nothing to aggregate. $\Sh$ has two resolutions, and the
difference between them is the only knob in this paper.

\begin{definition}[Operational reading $\Sbot$]
$\Sbot$ resolves $\Sh$ by head reduction. When computing $\Sh(n)$ returns to $n$
before exposing a constructor (an unproductive head cycle), the reduction loops;
we read this as $\Sh(n)$ undefined, i.e.\ $\bot$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Least-fixpoint reading $\Semp$]
$\Semp$ resolves $\Sh$ as the least fixpoint of its monotone recurrence, computed by
\emph{merging partial Berarducci trees} from $\bot$ upward
\citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}. The denotation at a position is a partial tree, a tree
carrying $\bot$ holes; each step exposes one more constructor layer and the result
is merged into the accumulator, filling holes, until the tree stops growing. Because
positions are interned, a position re-entered during its own computation is caught
by a pointer test, so an unproductive head cycle, which exposes no constructor
before returning, settles at the empty value $\emptyset$. Keeping a single
partial-tree value per position, rather than a set, is what keeps the merge
effective when a position could otherwise be reached in unboundedly many ways.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Locus of difference]\label{prop:locus}
$\Sbot$ and $\Semp$ agree on every node whose shape is either derivable in
finitely many steps or undefined for a reason other than an unproductive head
cycle. They differ exactly on nodes with an unproductive head cycle, where
$\Sbot$ gives $\bot$ and $\Semp$ gives $\emptyset$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
This is the classical gap between SLD termination and least-model membership for
definite programs \citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}: an atom is in the least model iff
it has a finite derivation; an unproductive cycle has no finite derivation
(least-model value $\emptyset$) but the unmemoised operational search loops
($\bot$). \TODO{state and prove precisely, fixing the notion of ``unproductive
head cycle''.}
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Codomains]\label{rem:codomains}
$\Sbot$ takes values in Berarducci trees with $\bot$. $\Semp$ takes values in
Berarducci trees with \emph{two} bottom-like leaves, $\bot$ and $\emptyset$,
which are distinct: $\bot$ is ``undefined / nonterminating'', $\emptyset$ is
``defined, but the empty set of shapes''. The extra leaf $\emptyset$ is precisely
where $\Semp$ exceeds $\BeT$.
\end{remark}

\label{sec:grounding}

\begin{theorem}[Presentation adequacy]\label{thm:adequacy}
For every term $M$, $\Btree(\subt{M}{\varepsilon}) = \BeT(M)$ under the
operational reading $\Sbot$.
\end{theorem}

The operational $\Sh$ is weak head reduction; the three constructor cases of
Definition~\ref{def:btree} are the three cases of the Berarducci tree
construction; retaining $\bot$-headed applications matches Berarducci's treatment
of root-stable applications with a meaningless head; and an undefined $\Sh$
(looping head reduction) is exactly a root-active term, i.e.\ a $\bot$ leaf. The
result follows by coinduction on the tree. \TODO{write out the coinductive

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\Semp$}{S\_empty}: the least fixpoint by merging partial trees}
\label{sec:valrec}
% =====================================================================
$\Semp$ is the least fixpoint of $\Sh$, computed by merging partial Berarducci
trees from $\bot$ upward (Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings}). Because positions are
interned, a position re-entered during the computation of its own shape is detected
by a pointer test, and the behaviour splits on whether that re-entry is productive.

\begin{definition}[Guarded vs.\ unproductive re-entry]
A position $n$ re-entered while computing $\Sh(n)$ is \emph{guarded} if a
constructor is exposed before the re-entry, and \emph{unproductive} if $\Sh(n)$
returns to $n$ with no constructor exposed.
\end{definition}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Guarded.} $\Sh(n)$ is a constructor and the re-entry sits in a
        child position. The merge folds at the re-entry, yielding a \emph{rational}
        (regular) infinite tree, finitely represented. This is the \emph{same} tree
        $\Sbot$ would unfold; $\Semp$ differs only in computing a finite
        representation, and terminating.
  \item \textbf{Unproductive.} $\Sh(n)$ returns to $n$ with no constructor exposed;
        its least fixpoint is $\emptyset$. Here $\Semp(n) = \emptyset$ whereas
        $\Sbot(n) = \bot$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{example}[Guarded: the cyclic stream $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$]\label{ex:cons}
Write the cyclic stream of zeros $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$ as $Y\,(\mathtt{cons}\;0)$,
with the Scott encoding $\mathtt{cons} = \lambda h.\lambda t.\lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,h\,t$.
Then $\Sh(r) = \lambda c.\lambda n.\,c\,0\,r'$, derived in finitely many steps
without re-requiring the head $\Sh(r)$: the recursive occurrence enters only as
data. The structurally repeating cell interns to one position, so the merge folds
at the (guarded) re-entry:
\[
  \rho \;=\; \lambda c.\,\lambda n.\,(c\cdot 0)\cdot \rho,
\]
the finite representation of the rational tree
$\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\lambda c.\lambda n.\,(c\,0)\,(\cdots))$.
$\Sbot(r)$ is the same infinite tree but has no finite representation, so its
enumeration diverges. No recursion binder is needed: $Y$ produces the repetition
that interning folds.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Unproductive: $\Omega$ and $Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$]\label{ex:xx}
$\Omega = (\lambda x.\,x\,x)(\lambda x.\,x\,x)$ head-reduces to itself with no
constructor exposed, returning to the same interned position; so $\Sbot(\Omega) =
\bot$ (head reduction loops) while $\Semp(\Omega) = \emptyset$ (the least fixpoint
of a base-less self-supporting position). The same holds for
$Y\,(\lambda x.\,x)$, the pure-$\lambda$ rendering of $\mathtt{letrec}\;x = x$.
These are pure $\lambda$-terms, so the two readings differ already on the pure
fragment: $\Semp$ is strictly more defined.
\end{example}

\section{Convergence Hierarchy}\label{sec:bridge}
% =====================================================================
$\Semp$ does not conservatively extend the Berarducci tree; it is strictly more
defined. The Berarducci reading $\Sbot$ and the least-fixpoint reading $\Semp$ sit
in a convergence hierarchy, agreeing on productive positions and parting at
unproductive cycles.

\begin{theorem}[Convergence hierarchy]\label{thm:bridge}
$\Semp \sqsupseteq \Sbot$. The two agree on every productive position: where head
reduction exposes a constructor, both read out the same Berarducci subtree, and
where head reduction would unfold a guarded cycle forever, $\Semp$ returns the
finite rational representation of that same tree. They differ exactly at
unproductive cycles, where $\Sbot = \bot$ and $\Semp = \emptyset$. The difference is
witnessed already on the pure fragment by $\Omega$ (Example~\ref{ex:xx}), whose
Berarducci tree is $\bot$ but whose $\Semp$ value is $\emptyset$; so $\Semp$ is
strictly more defined than $\BeT$, not a conservative extension of it.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
On a productive position the head is a constructor in finitely many steps, so both
readings expose it; a guarded re-entry folds under $\Semp$ to the finite
representation of the tree $\Sbot$ unfolds (Proposition~\ref{prop:locus},
Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy}). The two part only at an unproductive re-entry, where
$\Sbot$ loops ($\bot$) and the least fixpoint settles at $\emptyset$. $\Omega$ is
such a term and is pure, so the gap is nonempty on the pure fragment.
\TODO{state $\sqsubseteq$ on the tree domain precisely and prove the locus of
difference is exactly the unproductive re-entries.}
\end{proof}

\noindent
Equivalently: the Berarducci tree is what head reduction \emph{unfolds}, and $\Semp$
is what the merge \emph{folds}. Folding terminates on every rational structure and
assigns the empty value to an unproductive cycle, so it converges strictly more
often, which is why the hierarchy is proper.

\section{Applications}
\label{sec:application}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{Reading the empty value back into the calculus}
\label{sec:collector}
The least fixpoint (Section~\ref{sec:tworeadings}) gives each position a single
value: a shape, or $\emptyset$ at an unproductive cycle. To consume that inside the
$\lambda$-calculus the only choice is how $\emptyset$ reads out; a present shape is
forced to read out as itself. We encode $\emptyset$ as a first-class value, so that
emptiness is case-analysable with no external $\mathtt{isNil}$ primitive, while a
present value keeps its wrapper-free readout and so agrees with $\BeT$ on every
productive position (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}).

\paragraph{$\emptyset$ is a distinct leaf, and why the merge is sound}
Semantically $\emptyset$ is its own leaf, distinct from $\bot$ and from every
$\lambda$-term (Remark~\ref{rem:codomains}). This distinctness is exactly what makes
the least-fixpoint merge sound: its bottom is this fresh $\emptyset$, not a reused
value, so a position still sitting at $\emptyset$ is never mistaken for one that has
resolved. Were the bottom instead a genuine value such as the second Scott
constructor $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, the convergence test would become ambiguous
(a position at the placeholder is indistinguishable from one that truly computed
$\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$) and a round could even read a dependency's placeholder
as that real value and reach a \emph{wrong} fixed point. So $\Semp$ keeps
$\emptyset$ as its own leaf, and the readout of a present shape is the value itself.

\paragraph{An optional object-level encoding, under a side condition}
To consume absence \emph{within} the calculus one may encode $\emptyset$ as
$\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, so that the case analysis $w\;a\;b$ on a readout $w$
selects $b$ exactly when $w$ is absent, with a present value occupying the first
constructor ($\present = \lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,a$) and left unwrapped. This is the
type-safe comma-ok idiom, but it is sound \emph{only} under a side condition: the
term's present values must avoid the slot $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$, for otherwise
a genuine $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ and an empty cycle would be conflated and the
false branch would fire for both. A query whose present values are a single marker
$\present$ (reachability, Section~\ref{sec:reach}) satisfies it; in general
$\emptyset$ is kept as the distinct leaf above. Either way there is no aggregate and
no deduplication: the shape is single-valued, and the only test of identity, the one
that lets the merge fold a cycle, is a pointer comparison of interned positions,
not the undecidable $\beta$-equality of values.

\paragraph{Agreement on productive positions}
Because a present shape reads out as $v$ itself, with no wrapper, a term that
converges to $v$ still reads out to $v$, so $\Semp$ agrees with $\BeT$ on every
productive position (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}); the readings part only at the
unproductive cycles, where $\Semp$ is the more defined.
The empty readout $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ appears only where $\Semp$ already
differs from $\BeT$, namely at an unproductive cycle (Remark~\ref{rem:codomains});
giving that extra leaf this object-level body chooses a representation for it, it
does not introduce a new disagreement on the convergent fragment.

\paragraph{The comma-ok idiom}
The encoding is the type-safe form of the multiple-return convention of Go
($v,\mathit{ok} := m[k]$) and Lua: absence is signalled in a secondary channel
while the value itself is returned unwrapped. The difference is that a Scott case
is a sum, not a product: when the result is $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ there is no
value at all, so the consumer is forced to handle the absent branch rather than
silently reading a leftover zero value.

\paragraph{The knob, and full abstraction}\label{sec:variants}
The empty readout $\lambda a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ and the operational $\bot$ are the two
values the empty cycle may take, and they are the whole knob: the readings agree on
every present shape (each reads out as itself) and part only at $\emptyset$. The
choice $\emptyset = \bot$ is the one that lands on a classical tree equivalence.
Under it, by Theorem~\ref{thm:adequacy} the denotation of a term is its Berarducci
tree, and observation through the readout---place a term in a closing context and
watch whether the readout converges, exposing a head constructor---identifies two
terms exactly when their Berarducci trees coincide. Berarducci-tree equivalence is
thus \emph{fully abstract} for $\emptyset = \bot$: contextual equivalence equals tree
equality. Soundness is the easy direction; the content is completeness, that a
difference in the trees surfaces at some finite path and a context can drive the
readout to it. \TODO{state the contextual-equivalence relation precisely and prove
both directions; cite the B\"ohm-out technique adapted to the Berarducci setting.}

The least-fixpoint reading is strictly finer. Taking $\emptyset = \lambda a.\,\lambda
b.\,b$ tells an unproductive cycle (a value a context can case-split on) apart from
genuine divergence ($\bot$), so it distinguishes terms whose Berarducci trees agree;
hence $\Semp$ is \emph{not} fully abstract for Berarducci-tree equivalence. Full
abstraction is exactly the property $\emptyset = \bot$ has and $\emptyset = \lambda
a.\,\lambda b.\,b$ gives up, in exchange for the extra definedness of
Section~\ref{sec:application}. Both readings agree on every productive position for
the same reason: a present shape reads out unwrapped, so $\emptyset$ never arises
there and the choice is invisible; they part only at the unproductive cycles, where
$\Semp$ is strictly the more defined.

\subsection{Structural computation over cyclic data}
\label{sec:reach}
A recursive query over cyclic data is an ordinary $\lambda$-function. Take the naive
walk
\[
  \mathtt{reach} \;=\; Y\,\big(\lambda\mathit{self}.\,\lambda\mathit{lst}.\;
    \mathit{lst}\;(\lambda h.\lambda t.\,\mathit{self}\;t)\;\present\big),
\]
which recurses down a Scott stream looking for $\mathtt{nil}$. Applied to the cyclic
stream $r = \mathtt{cons}\;0\;r$, each recursive call builds the application
$\mathtt{reach}\;t$; because $r$ has finitely many interned positions, these
applications intern to finitely many positions, so the least fixpoint folds the
recursion. Head reduction, which interns nothing, builds a fresh application at every
step and diverges; $\Semp$ folds at the repeated interned position and returns a
definite value (here $\emptyset$, since no $\mathtt{nil}$ is ever reached). The query
terminates for the same reason the readout does: finitely many interned positions.
It is also memoised for free, since interning shares the repeated subcomputations.

\paragraph{Lazy, not eager}
The fixpoint is lazy, not eager: the same discipline lets a query \emph{produce} a
new cyclic datum. Mapping $r$ to a new stream isomorphic to it, with a transformed
head, the merge registers each new position before recursing into its tail, so the
result is a finite cyclic structure rather than an infinite unfolding. This
knot-tying is exactly the lazy least fixpoint; interning supplies the table.

\subsection{Memoising arbitrary programs}
\label{sec:memo}
More generally, $\Semp$ is the denotation of a \emph{memoised} run of any
$\lambda$-program. Memoisation needs a comparable key on which to recognise a
repeated call; the higher-order view offers none, since value equality of functions
is undecidable. The first-order reading replaces values by interned positions, whose
identity is a pointer test, so it is the natural setting in which memoisation is
even definable. On terminating computations memoisation is transparent; its only
effect is on a computation that loops by revisiting a position, where it returns the
least-fixpoint value rather than diverging, converging $\bot$ to $\emptyset$. This is
exactly the difference between $\Sbot$, the Berarducci tree that head reduction
unfolds with no memoisation, and $\Semp$, the least fixpoint that interning memoises:
$\Sbot$ is what you compute by remembering nothing, $\Semp$ what you compute by
remembering every position.

% =====================================================================
\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}
% =====================================================================
\paragraph{Trees and infinitary $\lambda$-calculus.}
The Berarducci, L\'evy--Longo, and B\"ohm trees form a hierarchy by their
meaningless sets \citeTODO{berarducci, levy-longo, barendregt}; the infinitary
$\lambda$-calculus framework \citeTODO{kennaway-klop-sleep-devries} situates them
uniformly. Our $\Sbot$ is the Berarducci tree (weak-head observation, retained
$\bot$-headed applications).

\paragraph{Least Herbrand models and tabling.}
$\Semp$ is the least Herbrand model of a definite program
\citeTODO{vanemden-kowalski}; the difference from $\Sbot$ is the SLD-termination /
least-model gap, and a tabled evaluator \citeTODO{tamaki-sato, tabling-slg}
computes $\Semp$ by completing unproductive cycles instead of looping. Tabling is
the canonical mechanism for terminating left-recursive and cyclic fixpoint queries
that plain resolution diverges on; our query application
(Section~\ref{sec:reach}) is an instance of it transported into the
$\lambda$-calculus.

\paragraph{Rational trees and recursive types.}
The capability of deciding structural queries over cyclic structures by
cycle detection is long established outside the $\lambda$-calculus. Rational
(regular) infinite trees and their algebra \citeTODO{courcelle} underlie cyclic
unification in logic programming \citeTODO{colmerauer}, where the occurs-check is
dropped and cyclic terms are first class. In type theory, equirecursive
(\emph{regular}) recursive types give terminating algorithms for subtyping and
equality precisely by detecting cycles in the type's regular tree
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. These are the same phenomenon as our
guarded-cycle case---a rational denotation with finitely many distinct
subtrees---reached by different routes. The point of difference is one of
position rather than capability: those approaches add machinery (a unification
discipline, a separate type-level system), whereas $\Semp$ is a re-reading of the
$\lambda$-calculus's own tree semantics, obtained by interning its positions and
taking a least fixpoint, with nothing added to the calculus
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}).

\paragraph{Cyclic $\lambda$-terms and recursion.}
Cyclic graphs / explicit recursion \citeTODO{ariola-klop} and the call-by-need
$\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{ariola-felleisen} provide the sharing on which the
$\Semp$/$\Sbot$ difference rests; the guarded-cycle case yields rational trees,
and the productivity distinction is the standard one from infinitary rewriting.

\paragraph{Sharing in functional languages.}
That a pure functional language cannot observe sharing or cyclicity is a known
obstacle: a traversal of a cyclic structure diverges because referential
transparency hides the repetition. Existing remedies \emph{add} something to the
language, such as observable sharing \citeTODO{gill} or the deliberate use of
laziness in circular programs \citeTODO{bird}. We add nothing to the calculus:
interning the positions exposes the repetition, and $\Semp$ folds it.

\paragraph{Higher-order model checking.}
Deciding properties of the (possibly infinite) trees generated by higher-order
programs \citeTODO{ong-hors, kobayashi} shares the goal of answering queries about
$\lambda$-generated infinite trees, but proceeds by automata and games over
B\"ohm-tree-like denotations rather than by a least-model reading of a shape
relation.

\paragraph{Lazy $\lambda$-calculus.}
The lazy $\lambda$-calculus \citeTODO{abramsky-ong} observes weak head normal
forms but, unlike the Berarducci reading, does not retain $\bot$-headed
applications; its tree is L\'evy--Longo, not Berarducci.

\paragraph{Defunctionalization.}
Unlike defunctionalization \citeTODO{reynolds}, which compiles a higher-order
program into a first-order interpreter, we give a first-order semantic
\emph{object} for the higher-order calculus; no interpreter is constructed.

\paragraph{Position.}
The capability we exploit---terminating structural computation over cyclic
data by cycle detection---is decades old in logic programming
\citeTODO{colmerauer, tamaki-sato} and in recursive-type theory
\citeTODO{amadio-cardelli, brandt-henglein}. What appears to be new is its
position: it is obtained by interning the positions of the $\lambda$-calculus's own
Berarducci-tree semantics and taking a least fixpoint, without leaving the calculus
or adding primitives. That least fixpoint is strictly more defined than the tree
(Section~\ref{sec:bridge}), terminating on every rational structure and assigning a
value to unproductive cycles; the prior first-order settings, lacking
$\beta$-reduction, have no comparable gap to open.

% =====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =====================================================================
The Berarducci tree is the readout of a first-order shape relation. Head reduction
unfolds that relation into the tree; the least fixpoint, computed by merging partial
trees over interned positions, folds it. The least-fixpoint reading is strictly more
defined: it terminates on every rational structure and assigns the empty value to an
unproductive cycle, so the two readings sit in a convergence hierarchy whose single
knob is the value an unproductive cycle takes.
